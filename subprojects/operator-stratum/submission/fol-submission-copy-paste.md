## Title

Why clause structure is judged like tense and agreement: public-update operators and grammaticality

## Short Running Head

Public-update operators and grammaticality

## Article Type

Article

## Abstract

Grammaticality judgments are selective: speakers reject some mismatches as ungrammatical while treating accent, register, and many lexical choices as negotiable. This article argues that the contrast tracks public-update operators: restricted form--value relations that configure what move an utterance counts as, who is committed to what, and which responses are licensed. Clause typing, argument linking, polarity, evidentiality, and grammaticalized tense--aspect occupy this role where they belong to closed, accountable repertoires. The account explains why clausal architecture patterns with tense and agreement, why tone, gesture, and lexical items can become grammatical, and why ideological policing differs from first-order operator mismatch. It predicts repair, preemption, and processing effects around operator contrasts cross-linguistically.
