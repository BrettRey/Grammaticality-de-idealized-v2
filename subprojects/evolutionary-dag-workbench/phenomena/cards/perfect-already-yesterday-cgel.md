# Phenomenon Card: present perfect with already and yesterday

**Stress test:** Definite past-time expression, current relevance, and temporal-anchor fit.

## Description

CGEL treats present perfect plus a definite past-time expression as normally excluded, but records a
restricted experiential-perfect case where `already` can change the fit of the construction:

- present perfect with bare definite past time: `*We have discussed it yesterday`;
- present perfect with `already` in a restricted experiential context: `We've already discussed it
  yesterday`;
- simple past with the same past-time expression: `We discussed it yesterday`.

The contrast prevents a graph from using a blanket ban on present perfect plus a past-time
expression. The issue is the interaction among present perfect, the kind of past-time anchor, and
the extra contribution of `already`.

## Why It Matters

The local `I have done it yesterday` card and the continuative-perfect card both exposed a missing
temporal-anchor construct. This card adds a sharper discriminator: the same definite past-time
expression can be impossible in the ordinary present-perfect frame but licensed under a narrower
experiential-perfect frame when `already` changes the comparison.

## Minimum Contrast Cells

- present perfect with bare definite past-time expression: `*We have discussed it yesterday`;
- present perfect with `already` plus definite past-time expression in a restricted experiential
  context: `We've already discussed it yesterday`;
- simple past plus definite past-time expression: `We discussed it yesterday`;
- recent-past perfect with indefinite recency expression: `She has recently/just been to Paris`.

## Potential Construct Pressure

This card strengthens pressure for a temporal-anchor or tense/aspect-alignment construct with
substructure for current relevance, experiential framing, and definite past-time anchoring. Broad
frame fit can record the contrast, but it cannot yet predict why `already` changes the status of the
`yesterday` expression.

## Graph Failure Modes

- Treats all present perfect plus past-time expressions as equally impossible.
- Treats the `already` case as mere semantic recoverability.
- Treats simple past and present perfect as interchangeable past-reference forms.
- Ignores the difference between definite past-time expressions and indefinite recency expressions.

## Predicted Discriminators

- `FRAME` should partly survive because it separates frame fit from recoverability.
- A future temporal-anchor graph should do better by representing the specific anchoring relation.
- `PROC` should not explain the contrast as processing difficulty.
