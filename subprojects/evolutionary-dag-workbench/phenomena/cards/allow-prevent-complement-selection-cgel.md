# Phenomenon Card: allow/prevent complement selection

**Stress test:** Catenative class, complement form, and neighbouring cohort variation.

## Description

CGEL separates catenative verbs by the form type of the non-finite complement they license. `allow`
patterns with to-infinitival complements in examples such as:

- `Will you allow me to audit your course?`
- `We mustn't allow there to be any repetition of this behaviour.`
- `The weather didn't allow us to finish the game.`

By contrast, `prevent`, `prohibit`, `preclude`, and one sense of `stop` pattern with
gerund-participial complements, with a related `from` construction:

- `He prevented us/our seeing her.`
- `He prevented us from seeing her.`

This card is not evidence that Brett's children-style `allowed doing` contrast is generally
licensed. It supplies the neighbouring CGEL baseline: catenative complement selection is
verb-class-specific and construction-sensitive, not a single free alternation.

## Why It Matters

The card blocks a graph from treating all catenative complement choices as either raw collocation or
free paraphrase. It also blocks the opposite overreach: treating a local cohort contrast as enough
to rewrite the whole catenative class. A useful graph must keep catenative class, constructional
meaning, cohort-conditioned production, and community licensing apart.

## Minimum Contrast Cells

- `allow` with object plus to-infinitival complement;
- `allow` with dummy `there` plus to-infinitival complement;
- `prevent` with gerund-participial complement;
- `prevent` with `from` plus gerund-participial complement;
- local cohort contrast for passive `allowed doing`, kept separate from the CGEL baseline.

## Potential Construct Pressure

This card strengthens pressure for a catenative-subclass or complement-form licensing construct. It
also shows that the `allowed doing` cohort card needs a bridge between local cohort production and
broader catenative class structure.

## Graph Failure Modes

- Treats `allow`, `prevent`, `prohibit`, `preclude`, and `stop` as one undifferentiated complement
  selection pattern.
- Treats `from` insertion as payload choice with no bearing on complement selection.
- Treats cohort-conditioned `allowed doing` production as proof that the CGEL baseline is wrong.
- Treats semantic recoverability as licensing.

## Predicted Discriminators

- `SEL` should partly survive because it represents selection but lacks catenative subclass
  machinery.
- `DYN` may become relevant only for cohort stabilization, not for the CGEL selection baseline.
- `PROC` should not explain the contrast as processing difficulty.
