# Phenomenon Card: frame-conditioned duration have

**Stress test:** Contextual anchoring without ad hoc licensing.

## Description

The answer `I have five years` fails as an answer to `How old are you?`, but succeeds as an answer to
`How long do you have left?`. The same surface form and duration value are available, but only one
question frame licenses the possession/duration construction as the intended reply.

## Why It Matters

The case tests whether a graph distinguishes context selecting among independently licensed frames
from context freely rescuing an otherwise wrong form-value relation. The bad age-answer version is
not uninterpretable, but recoverability of intended age does not license the `have + duration` frame
for ordinary English age ascription.

## Minimum Contrast Cells

- age-question frame: `How old are you?` / `I have five years`;
- remaining-duration frame: `How long do you have left?` / `I have five years`;
- age-question repair: `I am five years old` or `I am five`;
- duration-question paraphrase: `I have five years left`.

## Potential Construct Pressure

This card may require a construct for frame-conditioned constructional fit if `operator_value`,
`semantic_pragmatic_recoverability`, and `community_licensing` are too coarse to represent why the
duration reading is licensed in one reply frame but not the other.

## Graph Failure Modes

- Treats successful interpretation of the age answer as licensing.
- Treats context as able to create any intended form-value relation ad hoc.
- Treats the good duration answer as evidence that the bad age answer is also licensed.
- Ignores the question-answer frame as part of the contrast cell.

## Predicted Discriminators

- `OPG` should partly survive if it can keep recoverability separate from constructional licensing.
- `UPT` should help because the question frame configures the update role and response slot.
- `PROC` should not explain the contrast as processing difficulty.
- `DYN` should not explain the contrast through ideology or diachronic stabilization.
