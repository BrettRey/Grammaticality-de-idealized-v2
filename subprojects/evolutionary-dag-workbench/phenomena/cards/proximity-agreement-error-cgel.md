# Phenomenon Card: proximity agreement error

**Stress test:** Nearest-NP salience vs agreement licensing.

## Description

CGEL gives written examples where a singular subject NP contains a plural NP close to the verb and
the verb agrees with that closer plural NP:

- `The effect of the above resolutions are ...`
- `The accuracy of the quotes have not been disputed.`

CGEL treats such cases as ungrammatical processing errors, not as an English rule that the verb
agrees with the nearest preceding NP.

## Why It Matters

This card source-backs the nearest-agreement pressure. It separates a measurable production or
processing error from licensed agreement override constructions.

## Minimum Contrast Cells

- simple singular subject agreement;
- singular subject containing a plural nearest NP;
- first-pass or production error with plural verb;
- reread or edited correction back to the singular controller.

## Potential Construct Pressure

This card keeps pressure on a retrieval-attractor or agreement-controller construct. `PROC` can
represent the processing channel, but not the feature geometry that predicts which attractors cause
errors.

## Graph Failure Modes

- Treats proximity agreement errors as licensed overrides.
- Treats first-pass acceptability or production as community licensing.
- Ignores correction or rereading.
- Collapses proximity errors with notional or number-transparent agreement.
