# Phenomenon Card: preposition category and selection

**Stress test:** Lexical form, argument-linking selection, and payload preposition choice.

## Description

Prepositions are a boundary site: some choices are ordinary payload relations, while others are
tightly selected by heads or constructions and behave more like licensing conditions. A graph must
not treat every preposition choice as either free lexical selection or operator-like argument
linking.

## Source Pointers

- Source IDs: `cgel-prepositions`

## Why It Matters

This card directly pressures the earlier `depend on` / `depend of` problem. The workbench needs a
way to distinguish collocational rigidity from operator-like selection without overclaiming.

## Minimum Contrast Cells

- spatial or conceptual PP choice with open payload value;
- head-selected PP complement with a stable preposition;
- grammaticized preposition in a restricted construction;
- dialectal or historical competitor with limited licensing.

## Candidate Nodes

- operator_value
- constructional_analogy
- competitor_availability
- preemption_strength
- community_licensing
- reported_acceptability
- repair_reformulation_pressure

## Graph Failure Modes

- Treats all fixed prepositions as operator values.
- Treats all preposition choices as open lexical payload.
- Uses high frequency of one preposition without modelling competitors or opportunity.
- Cannot distinguish collocation from argument-linking licensing.

## Predicted Discriminators

- An operator-gap graph should pass only if it can name why the selected preposition is part of a
  restricted constructional repertoire.
- A usage-heavy graph should fail if it has only frequency and no selection/competitor structure.
