# Phenomenon Card: allowed to do / allowed doing cohort contrast

**Stress test:** Cohort-conditioned complement selection.

## Description

Brett reports the contrast:

- for Brett: `We're not allowed to do that` / `*We're not allowed doing that`;
- for his children: `We're not allowed to do that` / `We're not allowed doing that`.

The same predicate and intended prohibition are involved, but the complement pattern appears to be
licensed differently across household generations or cohort-linked repertoires.

## Why It Matters

The card is useful because it crosses two modules. It is a complement-selection case, so a graph
must not treat `doing that` as a free payload paraphrase of `to do that`. But it is also
cohort-conditioned, so a static selection graph should not declare the children's pattern
unlicensed simply because Brett's repertoire excludes it.

## Minimum Contrast Cells

- Brett/adult repertoire: `allowed to do` licensed;
- Brett/adult repertoire: `allowed doing` not licensed or strongly marked;
- children's/cohort repertoire: `allowed to do` licensed;
- children's/cohort repertoire: `allowed doing` licensed or at least not rejected in the same way;
- correction-framed adult response to children's `allowed doing`.

## Potential Construct Pressure

This card may require a future hybrid between selection structure and cohort-conditioned licensing:
either a graph that adds speaker/cohort and diachronic channels to selection, or a disciplined
evaluation comparing `SEL` and `DYN` without merging them prematurely.

## Graph Failure Modes

- Treats `allowed doing` as globally licensed or globally unlicensed.
- Treats cohort difference as mere error or immaturity without checking production stability.
- Treats complement selection as raw collocation.
- Treats adult correction as direct evidence of community licensing failure in the children's
  repertoire.

## Predicted Discriminators

- `SEL` should partly survive because it handles complement-selection structure.
- `DYN` should partly survive because it handles community/cohort and diachronic conditioning.
- Neither module alone should claim full coverage until production stability, correction patterns,
  and cohort-specific licensing are separated.
