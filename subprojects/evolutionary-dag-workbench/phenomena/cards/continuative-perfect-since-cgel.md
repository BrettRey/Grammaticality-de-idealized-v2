# Phenomenon Card: continuative perfect with since

**Stress test:** Perfect-tense anchoring and continuative aspectuality.

## Description

CGEL distinguishes non-continuative perfect uses from continuative perfect uses. A
continuative reading is supported by expressions such as `since` or `ever since`:

- `She has lived in Berlin ever since she married.`
- `*She lived in Berlin ever since she married.` under the intended ongoing reading.

The simple past can locate a situation wholly before the reference time, but it does not by itself
provide the continuative relation from before the reference time up to that reference time.

## Minimum Contrast Cells

- present perfect with `since`: `She has lived in Berlin ever since she married`;
- simple past with `since` under intended ongoing reading: `She lived in Berlin ever since she
  married`;
- present perfect without duration expression: `She has lived in Berlin`;
- simple past with wholly-before reading: `She lived in Berlin`.

## Potential Construct Pressure

This card strengthens pressure for a temporal-anchor or tense/aspect-alignment construct. A broad
frame-fit node can record the contrast, but cannot yet predict why `since` supports a continuative
perfect reading while the simple past fails under the same intended ongoing reading.

## Graph Failure Modes

- Treats the simple past and present perfect as interchangeable past-reference forms.
- Treats recoverability of the intended ongoing reading as licensing.
- Ignores the difference between continuative and wholly-before readings.
- Treats `since` as payload content with no effect on tense/aspect frame fit.
