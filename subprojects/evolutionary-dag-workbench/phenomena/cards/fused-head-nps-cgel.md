# Phenomenon Card: fused-head NPs

**Stress test:** Recoverability, fusion, ellipsis, and constructional licensing.

## Description

Fused-head noun phrases create recoverable forms whose licensing depends on a constructional
pattern rather than on recoverability alone. The hearer may know what omitted material would be,
but the grammar still distinguishes licensed fusion from an ad hoc missing-head analysis.

## Source Pointers

- Source IDs: `cgel-fused-head-nps`

## Why It Matters

The case tests whether graphs can represent a licensed construction without confusing semantic
recoverability with constructional licensing.

## Minimum Contrast Cells

- conventional fused-head NP;
- ordinary ellipsis licensed by a local discourse antecedent;
- recoverable but constructionally unsupported missing-head case;
- genre-bound abbreviated nominal expression.

## Candidate Nodes

- constructional_analogy
- semantic_pragmatic_recoverability
- community_licensing
- register_genre_appropriateness
- reported_acceptability
- grammaticality_attribution

## Graph Failure Modes

- Treats recoverability as sufficient for licensing.
- Treats all missing-head cases as ellipsis.
- Ignores constructional analogy and genre conditioning.
- Converts an analyst's category label into the target construct.

## Predicted Discriminators

- An operator-gap graph should pass only if it separates constructional support from recoverable
  meaning.
- A register/context graph should predict why some abbreviated nominal forms improve in headlines,
  notes, or labels without becoming generally licensed.
