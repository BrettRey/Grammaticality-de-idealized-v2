# Phenomenon Card: subject-verb agreement

**Stress test:** Operator contrast, notional agreement, and processing attraction.

## Description

Agreement cases separate several pressures: a closed agreement repertoire, semantic or notional
agreement, memory and attraction effects, and explicit correction. The same surface mismatch can
be treated differently when the trigger is a processing illusion, a collective/notional pattern, or a
stable variety-specific agreement system.

## Source Pointers

- Source IDs: `cgel-subject-verb-agreement`

## Why It Matters

Agreement is an easy place for graphs to overfit. A useful graph must not treat every agreement
anomaly as the same kind of grammatical state.

## Minimum Contrast Cells

- ordinary agreement in low-load clauses;
- attraction environment with an intervening plural;
- collective/notional agreement environment;
- correctness-framed judgment after rereading.

## Graph Failure Modes

- Treats processing attraction as a licensed agreement state.
- Treats notional agreement as a mere error.
- Uses reported acceptability as a direct proxy for operator licensing.
- Ignores rereading or task framing.

## Predicted Discriminators

- A processing-heavy graph should predict attraction-sensitive judgment shifts without licensing
  the attraction form.
- A context/dynamic graph should handle variety- or register-specific agreement patterns without
  reducing them to individual error.
