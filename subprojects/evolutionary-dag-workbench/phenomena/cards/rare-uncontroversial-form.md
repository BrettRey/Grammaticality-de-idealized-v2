# Phenomenon Card: rare uncontroversial form

**Stress test:** Low frequency vs licensing.

## Description

Some forms are rare because their opportunity contexts are rare, not because they are unlicensed.

## Source Pointers

- Source IDs: `reynolds-independent-relative-whose`, `reynolds-transparent-free-relatives`
- Related cards: [transparent-free-relatives](transparent-free-relatives.md),
  [fused-relative-constructions-cgel](fused-relative-constructions-cgel.md)

## Why It Matters

The case tests whether raw frequency is separated from opportunity mass and community licensing.

## Data Pointers

- The independent-relative-`whose` Bayesian analysis states the core discriminator: high
  opportunity plus systematic absence can support a strong negative posterior, but low opportunity
  plus absence should leave diffuse uncertainty.
- The independent-relative-`whose` experiment materials provide a minimal route from source pointer
  to measurement: compare low-opportunity critical items with dependent-`whose` and simple genitive
  baselines.
- Transparent-free-relative COCA materials provide a second route: use verified verb-class
  asymmetries and false-positive filtering to estimate whether absence is probative for a specific
  constructional subtype.
