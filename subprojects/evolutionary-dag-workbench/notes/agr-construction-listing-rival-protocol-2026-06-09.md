# AGR Construction-Listing Rival Protocol

**Date:** 2026-06-09
**AGR graph:** `graphs/archive/agreement-controller-override-candidate.json`
**Rival graph:** `graphs/archive/agreement-construction-listing-baseline-candidate.json`
**Status:** Rival registered; no new data, no score movement.

## Purpose

The current AGR COCA lane beats a simple surface-head-number baseline. That is not enough. A better
live rival is construction-specific listing:

> each lexical or constructional frame has a learned agreement preference, and no separate
> controller-identification, notional-basis, or licensed-override machinery is needed.

This protocol registers that rival so the AGR lane does not win by beating a baseline nobody should
care about.

## Rival Boundary

The construction-listing graph may accommodate observed cells once their local agreement profile is
known. It can say that `majority of people`, `a bunch of people`, or `a number of people` simply
has a learned plural preference in the relevant frame.

That is not yet a projective explanation. The live discriminator is generalization to low-frequency
or novel frames where item-specific listing has little or no stored preference to appeal to.

## Discriminating Tranche

Register a low-frequency/novel-QN tranche before inspecting any COCA cells:

- `a smattering of critics are/is`;
- `a gaggle of tourists are/is`;
- `a tranche of voters are/is`.

These are not claimed to be ideal final items. They are first runnable probes for whether AGR's
notional/controller path predicts agreement direction beyond already-listed central QNs. A stronger
future tranche can add elicited nonce items if corpus evidence is too sparse.

## Prediction Shape

AGR predicts that animate plural-member construal should support plural agreement availability or
dominance when the construction is used as a subject and the context profiles the members rather
than the group-as-unit.

The construction-listing baseline predicts only where a local preference is already stored or
recoverable from the item's own distribution. In low-frequency cells it should either default to
surface-head singular, remain uncertain, or require a new listed entry after the fact.

## Pass/Fail Boundary

AGR receives projection pressure only if the low-frequency/novel-QN tranche is registered before
data inspection and filtered results show a plural or context-conditioned profile that follows the
notional/controller diagnostics better than a listing/default account.

The construction-listing baseline remains live if the corpus cells are too sparse, if the items
show idiosyncratic profiles unrelated to complement animacy or construal, or if AGR requires
post-hoc item-specific exceptions.

No numeric score movement follows from registering this rival. The next data run should decide
whether the tranche is informative.
