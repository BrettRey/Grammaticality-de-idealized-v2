## Result

The candidate is not a licensing account. Its job is narrower: represent cases where processing
cost, recoverability, felt naturalness, measurement task, and task framing perturb reported
acceptability and grammaticality attribution without changing community licensing.

## Why This Mutation Was Warranted

The agreement evaluations showed that `context-aware-operator-gap-candidate` can partially handle
processing perturbation through `processing_cost -> reported_acceptability`, while `DYN` and `TASK`
are out of scope as attraction mechanisms.

The center-embedding evaluations repeated the same pattern and added a missing construct:
`felt_naturalness_context`. OPG has a direct processing-to-acceptability edge but no place for the
speaker's felt smoothness or ordinary-usability response. DYN and TASK can encode task and
attribution channels but not processing load or recoverability.

That repeated gap is enough to justify a scoped module. It is not enough to justify a general
processing theory of grammaticality.
