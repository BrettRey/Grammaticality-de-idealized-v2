# Discovery Rules

The workbench exists to discover better construct graphs, not to reproduce or justify OVMG, the
operator-stratum paper, detector models, usage-based accounts, processing accounts, or normative
accounts. Existing projects supply seed material, counterexamples, and comparison baselines only.

## Primary Target

The unit of progress is a graph that predicts, separates, or explains contrast cells better than its
predecessors. Agreement with an existing theory is neither evidence for nor evidence against a
graph unless it improves contrast-cell behaviour.

## Discovery Order

1. Start from phenomenon cards and contrast cells.
2. State the discriminating prediction before mutating a graph.
3. Add or split constructs only when a card forces the distinction.
4. Test the graph against adversarial cards that were not used to build the mutation.
5. Preserve scoped survivors without treating them as general winners.

## Anti-Alignment Rule

Penalize a graph when its rationale mainly preserves a familiar theory, paper, or named model rather
than improving prediction or construct separation. A graph can use `operator_configuration`,
`community_licensing`, `processing_cost`, or any other controlled construct, but it should not earn
standing because it resembles the project that introduced the construct.

## Naming Rule

New graph families should be named by the mechanism or contrast they model, not by the source paper
or preferred theory they resemble. Existing filenames may retain historical names for traceability,
but new candidates should use neutral slugs such as `context-feedback`, `measurement-dissociation`,
`opportunity-normalized-attestation`, or `processing-licensing-split`.

## Projectibility Rule

Coverage is not enough. A graph earns stronger standing only when it forecasts a contrast cell it
was not tuned on. Held-out prediction is the main route from useful map to evaluative framework.

## Score Consequences

- `projective_power` tracks held-out prediction and discriminating forecasts.
- `theory_preservation_penalty` tracks theory-saving moves that do not improve card behaviour.
- Numeric scores stay at zero until an authorizing protocol-bound or held-out evaluation justifies
  movement.
- A scoped-module label records limited survival, not endorsement of a source theory.
