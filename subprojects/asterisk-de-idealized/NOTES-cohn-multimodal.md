## Relevance to "De-idealizing the Asterisk"

### 1. The Principle of Equivalence Supports Modality-Neutral State Theory

Cohn argues that sound, gesture, and graphics share the same cognitive building blocks ("principle of equivalence"). This resonates directly with the MVMG/asterisk paper's claim that grammaticality is a **conditioned stability** property of form–value relations.

**Implication:** The state theory ($\widetilde{G}_t = \mathsf{map} \cdot K \cdot C_t$) should generalize across modalities. Visual languages (comics, diagrams) and signed languages would instantiate the same constitutive components, differing only in the sensory channel and the specific conditioning states $c$.

### 2. Omnia vs Semia: A Taxonomy for Partial Systems

Cohn distinguishes:
- **Omnia:** Modality + grammar + compositional meaning (spoken/signed/visual languages)
- **Semia:** Modality + meaning, but no robust grammar (emoji, co-speech gesture)

**Connection to the paper:** The asterisk paper's diagnostic tree (§3) implicitly applies only to omnia. Semia lack the recursive grammatical structure that generates stable-gap phenomena. This could be noted as a scope boundary: the paper's framework targets omnia; semia would require different opportunity-mass calculations since preemption dynamics differ without paradigmatic closure.

### 3. Conventionalization ≠ Signification Type

Cohn's Peircean correction:
- **Conventionalization:** Whether a form is regularized and shared (≈ $C_t$)
- **Signification type:** Icon/index/symbol (≈ part of $K$, the interpretive mapping)

**Implication for §2.5 (Indexicality):** The paper's treatment of socio-pragmatic indexicality could cite Cohn to clarify that indexical *value* is orthogonal to conventionalization. A form can be highly conventionalized and indexical (bathroom signs) or conventionalized and symbolic (most morphemes).

### 4. Testable Extension

If Cohn is right, the MVMG machinery should predict:
- Stable gaps in visual languages (e.g., systematically avoided panel transitions in comics)
- Processing-cost effects in visual narrative comprehension (analogous to centre-embedding)
- Satiation effects for marginal visual constructions

This could be flagged as future research in §8.
