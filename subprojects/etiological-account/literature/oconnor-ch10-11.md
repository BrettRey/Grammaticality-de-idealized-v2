# Notes on O'Connor (2019) - Chapter 10 & 11: Conclusion

### 1. SUMMARY OF THE ARGUMENT
*   **Categories:** Social categories (gender, race) emerge to solve coordination problems.
*   **Inequity:** Once categories exist, cultural evolution robustly generates inequitable conventions (the Cultural Red King, Bargaining Asymmetries).
*   **Robustness:** These results hold across many models (replicator dynamics, agent-based models, networks).

### 2. THE EPISTEMIC ROLE OF MODELS
*   **How-Possibly:** These models show *how* inequity *could* emerge from minimal conditions (no bias needed).
*   **How-Minimally:** They show the *minimum* required for oppression: just groups, learning, and slight asymmetry.

### 3. THE HYDRA
*   **Final Metaphor:** Inequity is a hydra. Because the structural conditions for inequity (power differences, coordination needs) persist, cutting off one head (e.g., passing a law) often leads to the emergence of new inequities elsewhere.
*   **The Future:** Understanding these dynamics is the first step to fighting the hydra effectively.
