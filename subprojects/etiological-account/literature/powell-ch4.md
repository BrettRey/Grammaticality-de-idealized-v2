# Notes on Powell (2020) - Chapter 4: The Critique from Convergent Evolution (CFC)

### 1. THE CRITIQUE FROM CONVERGENCE (CFC)
*   **The Argument:** Conway Morris and McGhee argue that **convergent evolution** (homoplasy) refutes Gould's RCT.
*   **Logic:** If life repeatedly evolves the same solutions (eyes, wings, sabertooth morphology) independently, then these outcomes aren't accidental. They are "nomically expectable."
*   **Metaphor:** McGhee compares biological forms to the Periodic Table. Just as physics forces atoms into specific stable configurations, biology forces life into a limited set of stable "spectrum of forms."

### 2. THE EERIE NATURE OF CONVERGENCE
*   **Examples:**
    *   *Thunniform Swimming:* Ichthyosaurs (reptiles), Tuna (fish), Sharks (cartilaginous fish), and Dolphins (mammals) all evolved the exact same teardrop shape and stiff tail for fast swimming.
    *   *Sabertooth:* Marsupials (Thylacosmilus) and Placentals (Smilodon) both evolved the "sabertooth syndrome."
*   **Implication:** These convergences suggest "attractors" in morphospace.

### 3. MISCONCEPTIONS OF GOULD
*   **Misconception 1: "Evolution never repeats."**
    *   *Correction:* Gould knew about convergence. The RCT doesn't deny repetition; it denies that the *overall body plans* are repeatable.
*   **Misconception 2: "Contingency = Unpredictability."**
    *   *Correction:* Gould admits local predictability (e.g., if you have a vertebrate, you can predict adaptations). He denies global predictability (e.g., that vertebrates would evolve in the first place).

### 4. GOULD'S DEFENSE: DEVELOPMENTAL FACILITATION
*   **Positive Constraints:** Internal developmental constraints don't just *prevent* change (negative); they *facilitate* certain pathways (positive).
*   **Channeling:** Once a lineage has a certain developmental toolkit (e.g., Hox genes), it is "channeled" toward certain convergent outcomes.
*   **The Point:** If convergence is caused by shared, contingent developmental biases ("deep homology"), then it doesn't refute the RCT. It's just a "Gouldian Repetition" (see Ch 5).

### 5. MACROEVOLUTIONARY OVERDETERMINATION
*   **Dennett's View:** If *Pikaia* died, something else would have evolved the "vertebrate trick." The outcome is "overdetermined."
*   **Powell's Critique:** This is unproven. We see convergence on *fins*, *wings*, and *eyes*, but we *never* see convergence on *body plans* (e.g., the vertebrate plan evolved only once). This asymmetry supports Gould.
