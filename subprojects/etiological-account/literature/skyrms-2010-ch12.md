# Notes on Skyrms (2010) - Chapter 12: Complex Signals and Compositionality

### 1. THE EVOLUTION OF SYNTAX
*   **The Question:** How do we get from one-word holophrastic signals ("Snake!") to complex signals ("Snake" + "Left")?
*   **Animal Precedents:** Birdsongs, monkey calls ("Boom" + "Alarm").

### 2. PRIMITIVE COMPOSITIONALITY
*   **Scenario:** Two senders. One sees vertical position (Up/Down), one sees horizontal (Left/Right).
*   **Reinterpretation:** Imagine these are the *same* sender seeing two different aspects of the world at once.
*   **Result:** A complex signal like "Red-Yellow" evolves.
    *   "Red" = Up.
    *   "Yellow" = Left.
    *   "Red-Yellow" = Up-Left.
*   **Compositionality:** The meaning of the whole is a function of the meaning of the parts. This evolves easily from standard reinforcement learning.

### 3. ORDER SENSITIVITY (SYNTAX)
*   **Temporal Order:** "Fly Left" then "Fly Right" is different from "Fly Right" then "Fly Left" (for a frog eating flies).
*   **Recruitment:** Signaling systems recruit this general-purpose temporal pattern recognition to build syntax.
*   **Example:** Campbell's monkeys use the "Boom" call *before* the alarm to modify it (creating a sentence-like structure).

### 4. CONCLUSION
*   There is no sharp line between human language and animal signaling. The rudiments of compositionality and syntax are present in nature and evolve for pragmatic efficiency.
