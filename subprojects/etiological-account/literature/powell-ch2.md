# Notes on Powell (2020) - Chapter 2: The Radical Contingency Thesis

### 1. COUNTERFACTUALS IN HISTORY
*   **The Problem:** We cannot run experiments on history. We must use counterfactuals ("What if Hitler died in 1930?").
*   **Necessity:** Despite skepticism, causal claims *require* counterfactuals. To say "The asteroid killed the dinosaurs" is to say "If the asteroid hadn't hit, the dinosaurs would have survived."

### 2. THE CAMBRIAN EXPLOSION & THE BURGESS SHALE
*   **Gould's Focus:** The Cambrian (541 Mya) saw the abrupt appearance of almost all animal phyla (body plans).
*   **The Weird Wonders:** Fossils like *Opabinia* (5 eyes, nozzle) and *Anomalocaris* seemed to fit no modern phylum. Gould argued they represented "disparate" plans that went extinct by chance.
*   **Pikaia:** A tiny chordate. If *Pikaia* had died (which was likely), no vertebrates, no humans.

### 3. THE TWO PILLARS OF RCT
Gould's thesis rests on two mechanisms:
1.  **Stochastic Extinction (Decimation):** Mass extinctions are "non-constructive." They don't select for "better" designs; they select for lucky ones (e.g., small size, burrowing, random geography).
    *   *Faunal Turnover:* The replacement of Brachiopods by Mollusks, or Dinosaurs by Mammals, wasn't a competitive takeover. It was luck (The K-Pg impact). Mammals weren't "better"; they were just lucky survivors.
2.  **Developmental Entrenchment:** Why don't we re-evolve the lost plans?
    *   *Kernels:* Upstream genetic regulatory networks (kernels) are causally connected to everything downstream. Changing them kills the embryo.
    *   *Result:* Body plans are "frozen." Evolution can only tinker with the details (downstream), not the fundamentals.

### 4. EMPIRICAL CRITIQUES OF GOULD
*   **Taxonomic Critique (Cladistics):** Modern cladistics reclassifies Gould's "weird wonders" (like *Opabinia*) as **stem-group** arthropods. They aren't "failed phyla"; they are just early branches of known groups.
    *   *Powell's Defense of Gould:* This is a nominal victory. Even if we call them arthropods, the *morphological disparity* they represented is gone. The contingency argument holds: if the stem-group leading to vertebrates died, we wouldn't be here.
*   **Ecological Critique:** New fossils (Chengjiang) show that chordates were more common/diverse than Gould thought. *Pikaia* wasn't a lone survivor; it had cousins.
    *   *Powell's Defense:* Prevalence helps survival, but doesn't guarantee it in a mass extinction. The K-Pg wiped out the incredibly dominant dinosaurs. The Cambrian could easily have wiped out the chordates.

### 5. CONCLUSION
*   Gould's core logic remains sound: **Mass extinctions are contingent filters**, and **developmental entrenchment prevents replays**. The specific history of life on Earth (the dominance of tetrapods, the loss of trilobites) is largely accidental and unrepeatable.
