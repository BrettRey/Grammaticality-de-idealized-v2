# Notes on Richerson & Boyd (2005) - Chapter 5: Culture Is Maladaptive

### 1. THE PARADOX OF MALADAPTATION
*   **The Observation:** Humans engage in spectacular maladaptations (from a genetic fitness perspective). The most glaring is the **Demographic Transition**: wealthy, successful moderns voluntarily limit their reproduction to below-replacement levels.
*   **Competing Explanations:**
    1.  **The "Big Mistake" Hypothesis:** Evolutionary Psychologists (like Tooby/Cosmides) argue our brains are adapted to the Pleistocene. Modern environments trigger these ancient mechanisms in wrong ways (e.g., we love sugar because it was rare; we seek status to get mates, but in modern economies, status seeking (careers) competes with mating).
    2.  **The Cultural Evolution Hypothesis (Authors' View):** Maladaptation is an inevitable byproduct of the *design* of culture. To get the benefits of social learning (speed/low cost), we must be "credulous." This opens the door to "selfish cultural variants" (memes) that spread at the expense of genes.

### 2. SELFISH CULTURAL VARIANTS
*   **Analogy to Pathogens:** Just as we have an immune system, we have selective biases. But just as pathogens evolve to trick the immune system, cultural variants evolve to trick our psychology.
*   **The Transmission Disadvantage:** Genetic fitness depends on *parents*. Cultural fitness depends on *social influence*.
    *   *Example:* A "career-first" meme. If teachers/professors are influential models but have few children, the "career-first" belief spreads through the population via non-parental transmission (school, media), even though it reduces genetic fitness.
*   **Runaway Selection:** Prestige bias can lead to "runaway" processes (like the peacock's tail). If people admire "giant yams" (Ponape) or "thinness" (modern West), they will pursue these goals to a maladaptive extreme to gain prestige.

### 3. THE DEMOGRAPHIC TRANSITION CASE STUDY
The authors argue the demographic transition is best explained by the spread of selfish cultural variants via non-parental transmission.

*   **Evidence:**
    *   **Princeton Fertility Project:** Fertility decline in Europe tracked *cultural/linguistic* boundaries, not just economic ones. (e.g., French speakers reduced fertility before German speakers in the same regions).
    *   **Modernization:** Modernization increases the influence of non-kin (teachers, media icons, peers) relative to kin. This favors memes that promote professional success/consumption over reproduction.
    *   **New Malthusianism:** Soap operas in developing nations (e.g., Brazil, India) depict wealthy, small families, actively spreading the "small family" meme.

### 4. RESISTANCE TO MALADAPTATION (THE ANABAPTISTS)
*   **The Amish & Hutterites:** These groups have resisted the demographic transition (maintaining huge families and high growth rates).
*   **How?** By creating a cultural "firewall."
    *   **Isolation:** They restrict exposure to mass media and higher education (limiting non-parental transmission).
    *   **Content Bias:** They devalue worldly status (prestige) and valorize manual labor and family life.
    *   **Result:** They act as a control group, showing that without modern cultural inputs, humans remain highly reproductive.

### 5. TRADEOFFS: SPEED VS. COMFORT
*   **Built for Speed:** Culture is an adaptation for rapid change. To work, it must be cheap and fast.
*   **The Cost:** If we rigorously checked every idea for its genetic fitness consequences, culture would be too slow to be useful (individual learning).
*   **Conclusion:** We are stuck with a system that usually works (adaptation) but occasionally kills us (maladaptation), because the machinery required to filter out *all* bad ideas would also filter out the good ones (like kayaks) that are too complex to validate individually.
