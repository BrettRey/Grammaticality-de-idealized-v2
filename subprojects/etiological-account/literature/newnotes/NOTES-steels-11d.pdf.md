# Notes on "Modeling the Cultural Evolution of Language" (Steels 2011)

**Author:** Luc Steels
**Source:** *Physics of Life Reviews* 8 (2011) 339–356
**File:** `steels-11d.pdf`

## Abstract
This paper reviews the field of evolutionary linguistics, specifically focusing on **computer simulations** and **robotic experiments** (Language Games). It contrasts the "biolinguistic" (Chomskyan) view with the "evolutionary/cultural" view.

## Core Perspective: Language as a Complex Adaptive System (CAS)
*   Language is not a static code or a purely biological organ.
*   It is a **distributed** system maintained by a population.
*   It evolves via **cultural selection** based on communicative success and cognitive effort.

## The Mechanisms: Language Games
Steels advocates for "Language Games" as the microscopic mechanism of change.
1.  **Interaction:** Two agents (Speaker, Hearer) attempt to communicate a goal (e.g., name an object).
2.  **Feedback:** Success or failure is determined (did the hearer pick the right object?).
3.  **Alignment/Update:**
    *   *Success:* Strengthen the association (reward).
    *   *Failure:* Weaken the association, or invent a new word/form (lateral inhibition).
    *   *Result:* Self-organization of a shared lexicon and grammar without a central coordinator.

## Key Domains Modeled
1.  **Vocabulary:** The "Naming Game." How a shared set of labels emerges from chaos (synonymy dampening).
2.  **Color Categories:** How continuous sensory spaces are partitioned into discrete categories. Cultural drift can shift these boundaries.
3.  **Grammar:**
    *   Emergence of *Case Grammars* (marking roles like Agent/Patient).
    *   Emergence of *Agreement Systems*.
    *   Grammar arises to reduce ambiguity and cognitive effort in complex communication.

## Contrast with Nativism
*   **Chomsky/Pinker:** Posit a "Language Acquisition Device" (LAD) with innate parameters. Evolution explains the LAD.
*   **Steels/CAS:** Posit general cognitive mechanisms (pattern recognition, shared attention, recursive processing). Structure emerges from the *interaction* dynamics (cultural evolution).
*   **"Fluid Construction Grammar" (FCG):** The formalism used by Steels to model these dynamic, learning-based grammars.

## Conclusion
The "cultural" hypothesis is sufficient to explain the origins of linguistic complexity. We do not need to postulate a "macromutation" for grammar. Language emerges from the recruitment of general cognitive capacities for the purpose of social coordination.
