# Notes on "Cultural Evolution" (NIHMS 747020)

**Author:** Alex Mesoudi
**Source:** NIHMS 747020 (Published in *Encyclopedia of Mental Health*, 2nd Edition, Vol 1, pp. 367-371, 2016)
**File:** `nihms-747020.pdf`

## Summary
This is a concise encyclopedia entry summarizing the field of **Cultural Evolution**. It frames culture as a Darwinian process and outlines the implications for understanding human behavior and mental health.

## Key Definitions
*   **Culture:** Information (knowledge, beliefs, skills, attitudes) acquired from others via social learning (imitation, teaching, language).
*   **Cultural Evolution:** The theory that cultural change follows Darwinian principles:
    1.  **Variation:** diverse beliefs/behaviors.
    2.  **Inheritance:** social transmission.
    3.  **Differential Fitness:** some traits spread more effectively than others.

## Core Concepts
1.  **Parallels with Biology:** Culture exhibits "descent with modification." Languages and artifacts can be analyzed using phylogenetic trees.
2.  **Forces of Cultural Change:**
    *   **Selection:**
        *   *Natural Selection:* Cultural traits affecting biological survival (e.g., smoking).
        *   *Cultural Selection:* Traits spreading due to psychological preferences (biases).
    *   **Biases:**
        *   *Content Bias:* Preferring simple/memorable traits.
        *   *Model-based Bias:* Copying prestigious/successful individuals.
        *   *Frequency-dependent Bias:* Conformity (copying majority).
3.  **Gene-Culture Coevolution:** Culture shapes the biological environment, driving genetic selection (e.g., lactose tolerance evolving due to dairy farming; cooperative psychology evolving due to cultural group selection).

## Relevance to Mental Health
The paper explicitly connects cultural evolution to mental health issues:
*   **Suicide:** Example of a maladaptive cultural trait spreading via imitation ("Werther effect").
*   **Anorexia/Bulimia:** Social transmission of body image ideals and eating behaviors, potentially driven by prestige bias (copying celebrities).
*   **Modern Mismatches:** Our evolved social learning mechanisms (e.g., prestige bias) might misfire in the modern mass-media environment, causing the rapid spread of maladaptive behaviors.

## Conclusion
Cultural evolution provides a synthetic framework for the social and biological sciences, explaining how human psychology and culture mutually shape each other.
