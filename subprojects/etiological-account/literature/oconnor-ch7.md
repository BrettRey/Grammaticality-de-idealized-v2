# Notes on O'Connor (2019) - Chapter 7: Discrimination and Homophily

### 1. THE PROBLEM
*   **Observations:** Women in academia get less credit for co-authored work. Women also tend to co-author with other women.
*   **The Link:** O'Connor argues these are causally linked. Discrimination leads to **homophily** (preferring in-group interaction).

### 2. TAKING THE OUTSIDE OPTION
*   **Model:** Nash Demand Game with an "Outside Option" (working alone).
*   **Result:** If a group faces discrimination (getting the Low payoff in collaboration), they are more likely to take the Outside Option.
*   **Implication:** Disadvantaged groups "opt out" of collaboration, even if collaboration would be more productive than working alone, because the *division* of the spoils is unfair.

### 3. EVOLVING NETWORKS
*   **The Model:** Agents on a network play bargaining games. They can break links with partners who give them low payoffs and form new links.
*   **Emergence of Homophily:**
    *   If Type A discriminates against Type B (demands High), Type B breaks the link.
    *   Type B forms links with other Type Bs (who treat them fairly).
    *   Result: The network spontaneously segregates. **Total Homophily**.
*   **Co-Evolution:** Even if bargaining strategies evolve simultaneously, partial homophily emerges. Agents keep fair cross-group links and drop unfair ones.

### 4. PRODUCTIVITY DIFFERENCES (BIGGER PIES)
*   **Scenario:** What if one group is more productive (e.g., has more grant money)?
*   **The Tradeoff:** The disadvantaged group might tolerate unfairness (getting a small slice) if the "pie" is huge (collaborating with a famous professor).
*   **Result:** Homophily breaks down. Inequitable integration occurs.

### 5. EPISTEMIC CONSEQUENCES
*   **Diversity in Science:** Philosophy of science suggests diversity improves inquiry.
*   **The Trap:** If discrimination leads to homophily (segregation), science loses the benefit of diverse teams.
*   **Intervention:** Incentivizing diverse collaboration might just expose minority scientists to more exploitation (Red King effects) unless the underlying bargaining norms change.
