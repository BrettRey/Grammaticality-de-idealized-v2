# Notes on O'Connor (2019) - Chapter 8: The Cultural Evolution of the Household

### 1. THE HOUSEHOLD AS A BARGAINING UNIT
*   **The Household:** A fundamental economic unit. Division of labor and resources happens here.
*   **The Puzzle:** Why is the household often a site of gender inequality? Why do women have less bargaining power?

### 2. THE GENDER WAGE GAP
*   **Feedback Loop:**
    *   If women earn less in the market, their "disagreement point" (fall-back option) in household bargaining is lower.
    *   This forces them to accept a worse deal in the household (more chores, less leisure).
    *   Doing more chores makes them less competitive in the market, reinforcing the wage gap.
*   **Evolutionary Model:** O'Connor models this as two coupled games (Market Game and Household Game).
    *   *Result:* Cultural evolution robustly drives populations to this "dual inequity" equilibrium.

### 3. DIVORCE AND THREAT POINTS
*   **Divorce:** The threat of divorce determines bargaining power.
*   **Asymmetry:** If men have better re-marriage prospects (e.g., age norms) or higher incomes, their threat point is higher. This leads to men demanding "High" in household resource division (Cultural Red King effect).

### 4. DOMESTIC VIOLENCE
*   **Violence as Strategy:** Violence can be modeled as a way to forcibly lower the partner's disagreement point or destroy their outside options (control).
*   **Evolution:** Even if violence is costly, if it succeeds in extracting more resources, it can evolve as a strategy in power-asymmetric relationships.

### 5. CONCLUSION
*   The household is not a haven from strategic conflict. It is a primary site where gender inequality is culturally evolved and maintained through bargaining asymmetries.
