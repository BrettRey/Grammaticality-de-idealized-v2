# Notes on Powell (2020) - Chapter 5: Convergent Evolution as Natural Experiment

### 1. NATURAL EXPERIMENTS
*   **The Problem:** We can't actually replay the tape (N=1). We must use "natural experiments"—cases where nature set up similar conditions.
*   **Validity:** A valid natural experiment requires **independence**. If the replicates are not independent, they don't prove robustness.

### 2. THE ANOLIS LIZARD PROBLEM
*   **The Study:** Losos et al. showed that *Anolis* lizards on different Caribbean islands independently evolved the exact same "ecomorphs" (Twig, Trunk-Ground, Crown-Giant).
*   **Interpretation:** This looks like strong convergence (RRT).
*   **Powell's Critique:** These lineages are geographically isolated but **developmentally identical**. They share the same *Anolis* genome and body plan.
*   **Conclusion:** This proves that *given* the Anolis plan, selection drives specific outcomes. It does *not* prove that the Anolis plan itself is robust. It is a "shallow replay."

### 3. GOULDIAN REPETITION VS. TRUE CONVERGENCE
*   **Gouldian Repetition:** Iteration caused by **shared developmental constraints** (homology).
    *   *Example:* Parallel evolution of pelvic spine loss in stickleback fish (driven by the same *Pitx1* gene mutation).
    *   *Significance:* Consistent with RCT. The outcome is "channeled" by a contingent history.
*   **True Convergence:** Iteration caused by **selection** overcoming disparate developmental starting points.
    *   *Example:* Camera eyes in Vertebrates vs. Cephalopods (though even here, *Pax6* is shared).
    *   *Significance:* Supports RRT. Shows the solution is a "cosmic imperative."

### 4. PARALLELISM AND DEEP HOMOLOGY
*   **Deep Homology:** Many traits (eyes, limbs, hearts) are regulated by ancient "master control genes" (e.g., *Pax6*, *distal-less*, *tinman*) shared by all bilaterians.
*   **The "Old Chestnut":** Does this make them parallelisms (homologies) rather than convergences?
    *   *Powell's View:* Yes, to a degree. If the "machinery" is shared, the independence is compromised.
    *   *Specific vs. General Causes:*
        *   *General:* *Pax6* says "make an eye here." (Shared trigger).
        *   *Specific:* Downstream genes determine "make a *camera* eye vs. *compound* eye." (Disparate implementation).
        *   *Verdict:* If only the trigger is shared, it's True Convergence. If the downstream specific machinery is shared, it's Gouldian Repetition.

### 5. THE LUMPING PROBLEM
*   **Critique of CFC:** Conway Morris lumps everything together. "Ant mimicry" evolved 70 times!
*   **Powell's Retort:** If the "Ant" phenotype is contingent, then "Ant Mimicry" is also contingent on that first accident. High frequency (70 times) doesn't mean "cosmically robust" if the target (Ants) is rare.
