# Notes on Skyrms (2010) - Chapter 6: Deception

### 1. DEFINING DECEPTION
*   **Misinformation:** A signal that moves the probability of the true state *down* (or the probability of a false state *up*).
    *   *Example:* Kitui (a vervet monkey) giving a "leopard" alarm when no leopard is present.
*   **Deception:** Systematic misinformation that benefits the sender at the expense of the receiver.
*   **Philosophical Skepticism:** Some philosophers argue deception is impossible in natural signaling because signals just "mean" their causes. Skyrms rejects this: if a signal statistically raises the probability of X, but X is not present, it is misinformation.

### 2. EXAMPLES IN NATURE
*   **Kitui (Vervet Monkey):** Uses false alarms to break up fights or deter rivals.
*   **Fireflies (*Photuris*):** Females mimic the mating flashes of another species (*Photinus*) to lure males in and eat them. This is clear deception.
    *   *Analysis:* The signal raises the probability of "Mate" (benefiting sender, hurting receiver) even though the true state is "Predator."

### 3. HALF-TRUTHS
*   A signal can raise the probability of *both* the true state and a false state.
*   *Firefly Example (Honest male):* When a male *Photinus* signals, it raises the probability of "Mate" (true) but also raises the probability of "Predator" (since predators track the signal).
*   **Deception?** Only if the sender benefits at the receiver's expense. The predator mimics benefit; the honest males do not (they just take a risk).

### 4. CAN DECEPTION BE UNIVERSAL?
*   **Kant's View:** Lying cannot be a universal law because if everyone lied, no one would believe it (signals would lose meaning).
*   **Game Theory View:**
    *   *Pure Conflict (Zero-Sum):* In equilibrium, signals carry *zero* information. Deception is impossible because no one listens.
    *   *Mixed Interest:* Partial information transmission is possible.
*   **Universal Deception Equilibrium:** Skyrms constructs a game where *every* signal is deceptive (a half-truth that leads the receiver to a suboptimal act) and yet it is an equilibrium.
    *   *Scenario:* Receiver does Act 3 given Signal 1, and Act 4 given Signal 2.
    *   *Outcome:* Receiver gets a payoff of 8 (suboptimal, could get 10 with perfect info). Sender gets 10 (optimal).
    *   *Stability:* Receiver still prefers 8 to the alternative (0), so they keep listening. Sender prefers 10, so they keep deceiving.
    *   *Conclusion:* **Kant was wrong.** Universal deception (via manipulation/half-truths) can be stable.

### 5. CYCLES OF DECEPTION
*   In games with opposed interests, the dynamics often don't settle into equilibrium. They form **cycles** (like Rock-Scissors-Paper).
*   *Process:* Senders evolve to deceive -> Receivers evolve to detect -> Senders evolve new lies -> Receivers become skeptical -> Senders tell truth (to get noticed) -> Receivers listen -> Senders deceive...
