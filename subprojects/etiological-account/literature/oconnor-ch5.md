# Notes on O'Connor (2019) - Chapter 5: Power and the Evolution of Inequity

### 1. THE EXPLANANDUM: WHY INEQUITY?
*   **The Question:** Why do some social groups get more and others less?
*   **Standard Answer:** Psychology (bias, stereotyping).
*   **O'Connor's Answer:** While psychology matters, **dynamical forces** alone can generate inequity even without bias. Inequity is a robust outcome of cultural evolution in populations with power asymmetries.

### 2. THE NASH DEMAND GAME
*   **The Model:** Two players divide a resource (e.g., value 10). They make demands (Low, Med, High).
    *   *Compatible:* If Sum(Demands) <= 10, they get what they asked for.
    *   *Incompatible:* If Sum > 10, they get the "Disagreement Point" (usually 0).
*   **Equilibria:**
    *   *Fair:* Med vs. Med (5, 5).
    *   *Unfair:* High vs. Low (7, 3) or Low vs. High (3, 7).
*   **With Types:** In a homogeneous group, fairness (5, 5) is the most likely outcome. But with **types**, unfair equilibria (Type A gets High, Type B gets Low) become stable conventions.
    *   *Result:* Simulations show that discriminatory conventions emerge >50% of the time just by adding types.

### 3. POWER AS THREATS (THREAT POINTS)
*   **Definition:** Power is the ability to walk away with more (higher disagreement point).
*   **The Model:** Player 1 gets T (e.g., 2) if bargaining fails. Player 2 gets t (e.g., 0).
*   **Evolutionary Result:** The group with the higher threat point (T) is *much* more likely to end up demanding High (the advantaged position) in the bargaining convention.
    *   *Mechanism:* High threat points reduce the risk of demanding High. If you demand High and crash, you still get T. If your opponent crashes, they get 0. This makes the powerful group "learn" aggressive strategies faster.
*   **Real World:** Men often have better fall-back positions (higher wages, physical threat) than women, leading to conventions where men demand more.

### 4. OUTSIDE OPTIONS
*   **The Model:** Instead of a threat point (failure payoff), actors have an "Outside Option" (payoff for opting out of bargaining entirely).
*   **Result:** If one group has a better outside option, the equilibria where they receive Low payoffs *disappear*. They simply won't play. This forces the cultural evolution toward equilibria where they get High or Med.

### 5. COMPOUNDING POWER (FEEDBACK LOOPS)
*   **The Logic:** Power begets resources. Resources beget power.
*   **The Model:** Start with equal groups. If Group A wins a bargain (gets High), their Threat Point increases for the *next* game.
*   **Result:** Small random fluctuations lead to one group gaining a slight advantage, which then snowballs into permanent dominance.
    *   *Initial Asymmetry:* If one group starts with even a *tiny* advantage, the feedback loop almost guarantees they become the dominant class.
*   **Conclusion:** Persistent inequality doesn't require innate superiority. It can result from chance events amplified by feedback loops.
