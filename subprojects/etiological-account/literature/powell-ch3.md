# Notes on Powell (2020) - Chapter 3: A Philosophical Theory of Evolutionary Contingency

### 1. DEFINING CONTINGENCY (THE AMBIGUITY)
*   **The Problem:** Gould never precisely defined "contingency." It has two common meanings:
    1.  **Causal Dependence:** Effect E depends on Cause C. (Beatty's first definition).
        *   *Critique:* This is too broad. Everything depends on causes. Even lawful events (like gravity) involve causal dependence.
    2.  **Unpredictability:** We couldn't predict the outcome.
        *   *Critique:* This is an *epistemic* issue (about our knowledge), not a *metaphysical* one (about the world). A chaotic deterministic system is unpredictable but not contingent in the relevant sense.

### 2. POWELL'S DEFINITION: "RADICAL CONTINGENCY"
*   **Modal Thesis:** The RCT is a claim about *modality* (possibility/necessity). It asks: "How stable is this outcome across possible evolutionary worlds?"
*   **Definition:** An outcome O is **radically contingent** if a *marginal* change in initial conditions would lead to a *radically disparate* outcome in any physically possible world.
*   **Sensitive Dependence:** This is akin to the "Butterfly Effect" in chaos theory. Small inputs -> Big differences.

### 3. THE "NOMOLOGICAL VACUUM" (LAWS OF BIOLOGY)
*   **The Debate:** Do biological laws exist?
    *   *Beatty/Rosenberg:* No. Biology is full of exceptions and accidents (e.g., the genetic code is a "frozen accident").
    *   *Sober/Powell:* Yes, but they are often "ceteris paribus" laws or "accidental non-accidental generalizations" (laws that hold *given* a contingent starting point).
*   **Powell's Stance:** The existence of general biological laws (like Natural Selection) doesn't refute RCT. The question is whether there are specific laws of *form* (e.g., "Intelligent life must be humanoid").

### 4. FRAMING THE ANTITHESIS: ROBUST REPLICABILITY (RRT)
*   **The Rival View:** The "Robust Replicability Thesis" (defended by Conway Morris).
*   **Claim:** Rerunning the tape from *disparate* starting points leads to highly similar outcomes.
*   **Mechanism:** **Strong Adaptationism**. Natural selection is so powerful it overrides historical constraints and drives lineages to global optima ("Good Tricks").
*   **The Test:** To prove RRT, one must show that selection drives lineages to the same solution despite different developmental starting points.
