# Notes on Powell (2020) - Chapter 6: The Entanglement Problem

### 1. THE HUMANOID FALLACY
*   **Science Fiction:** Aliens are almost always humanoid (Star Trek).
*   **Bundling Bias:** We assume that "Intelligence" is bundled with "Humanoid Body Plan" (bipedal, two hands, binocular vision).
*   **The "Cephalopoid":** In *Arrival* (2016), aliens are "Heptapods" (squid-like). This avoids the humanoid fallacy but creates a "Cephalopoid" bundle (Intelligence + Tentacles + Ink).
*   **The Goal:** We must **disentangle** the *robust* properties (Intelligence? Vision?) from the *contingent* properties (Bipedalism? Ink sacs?).

### 2. THREE DIMENSIONS OF ITERATION
To assess whether a convergence is "cosmically projectible," Powell proposes three criteria:
1.  **Specificity:** Is the trait specific (e.g., "Camera Eye") or vague (e.g., "Light Sensing")?
    *   *Rule:* Specific iterations are stronger evidence for RRT. Vague ones are trivial.
2.  **Independence:** Are the lineages developmentally distant?
    *   *Rule:* Distance matters. Convergence between Plants and Animals (e.g., fluid transport) is stronger than between Rats and Mice.
3.  **Scope:** Are the limiting conditions (e.g., light, water) universal?
    *   *Rule:* Traits dependent on rare co-evolutionary partners (e.g., Fig Wasps needing Figs) have low scope. Traits dependent on physics (e.g., Hydrodynamics) have high scope.

### 3. CASE STUDY: THE EYE
*   **Score:**
    *   *Specificity:* High. Camera eyes and Compound eyes are specific optical solutions.
    *   *Independence:* High. Evolved in Vertebrates, Mollusks, Arthropods, Annelids, and even single-celled Dinoflagellates (Warnowiids).
    *   *Scope:* High. Light is universal.
*   **Conclusion:** The **Image-Forming Eye** is a robust, cosmically projectible trait. We should expect aliens to have eyes.

### 4. REPLAY DEPTH
*   **Concept:** How far back can you rewind and still get the result?
*   **Shallow Replay:** Anolis Ecomorphs. (Requires Anolis ancestor).
*   **Moderate Replay:** Sabertooths. (Requires Mammalian ancestor).
*   **Deep Replay:** Eyes, Flight. (Requires only Multicellularity + Physics).
*   **Implication:** Gould was right about body plans (Shallow/Moderate stability). Conway Morris is right about functional devices (Deep stability).

### 5. THE ENTANGLEMENT
*   **The Problem:** We can't always separate the two.
*   **Example:** Is "Human-level Intelligence" a Deep Replay trait (like eyes) or a Shallow Replay trait (like the vertebrate skeleton)?
*   **Part II Preview:** The rest of the book uses the "Eye" case study to argue that **Cognition** is likely a Deep Replay trait.
