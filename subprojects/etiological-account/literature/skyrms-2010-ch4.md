# Notes on Skyrms (2010) - Chapter 4: Evolution

### 1. DARWINIAN EVOLUTION
*   **Three Essentials:** Natural Variation, Differential Reproduction, Inheritance.
*   **History:** These ideas predate Darwin (Empedocles, Lucretius, Hippocrates), but Darwin got the mechanism of selection right (if not inheritance).

### 2. EVOLUTIONARY GAME THEORY
*   **Maynard Smith & Price:** Introduced **Evolutionarily Stable Strategies (ESS)**.
    *   *Definition:* A strategy is ESS if, when common, it cannot be invaded by a rare mutant.
*   **Replicator Dynamics:** A dynamic model where the growth rate of a strategy is proportional to its fitness relative to the average.

### 3. EXAMPLES
*   **Hawk-Dove:** No pure ESS. The population evolves to a mixed equilibrium (polymorphism).
*   **Stag Hunt:** Two pure ESSs (All Stag or All Hare). The "mixed" point is an unstable tipping point. History determines which one you get.
*   **Prisoner's Dilemma:** One ESS (All Defect).
    *   *Note:* Correlation (e.g., kin selection) can change the game so cooperation becomes an ESS.

### 4. THE RED QUEEN (ROCK-SCISSORS-PAPER)
*   **Intransitivity:** Rock beats Scissors beats Paper beats Rock.
*   **Dynamics:** In the standard Replicator Dynamics, this creates **cycles**. The population orbits the center (1/3, 1/3, 1/3) but never settles.
    *   *Real World Examples:* Lizards (Side-blotched), Bacteria (*E. coli* strains of poisoners vs. immunes vs. sensitives).
*   **Structural Instability:** Small changes to the game (e.g., costs) can change the dynamics from closed cycles to inward spirals (stability) or outward spirals (instability).

### 5. REPLICATOR-MUTATOR DYNAMICS
*   **Adding Mutation:** Adding a small mutation rate (error/experimentation) changes the dynamics.
*   **Stabilization:** In Rock-Scissors-Paper, mutation pushes the population toward the center, turning cycles into stable inward spirals.
*   **Lesson:** "Structural stability" matters. A model that is unstable (like pure replicator dynamics in RSP) might be misleading if real-world factors (like mutation) stabilize it.
