# Notes on Skyrms (2010) - Chapter 1: Signals

### 1. THE ORIGIN OF SIGNALS
*   **The Question:** How do signaling systems arise spontaneously without a pre-existing language or intentions?
*   **Historical Views:**
    *   *Adam Smith/Vitruvius/Democritus:* Signaling is conventional and arises by chance or habit from individual interaction.
    *   *Alternative:* Innate (miraculous) signaling.
*   **Modern View:** Signaling exists at all levels of biology (bacteria, birds, monkeys, cells). The goal is to explain the spontaneous evolution/learning of these systems using game theory.

### 2. SENDER-RECEIVER GAMES (DAVID LEWIS)
*   **The Model:**
    *   **Sender:** Observes a state of the world (Nature). Sends a signal.
    *   **Receiver:** Observes the signal (not the state). Chooses an act.
    *   **Payoff:** Both get a payoff if the Act matches the State (Common Interest).
*   **Signaling System:** An equilibrium where information is transmitted perfectly (Act always matches State).
*   **Conventionality:** Signals have no intrinsic meaning. Meaning arises from the equilibrium. Permuting messages leads to another valid equilibrium.

### 3. INFORMATION
*   **Quantity:** Measured by how much a signal changes probabilities (Kullback-Leibler).
    *   *Info about State:* Sender's use of signals to discriminate states.
    *   *Info about Act:* Effectiveness of signal in changing receiver's action.
*   **Content:** "The word is the shadow of the act" (Democritus). Meaning is defined by function in the equilibrium.

### 4. EVOLUTION (REPLICATOR DYNAMICS)
*   **Dynamics:** A model of differential reproduction or imitation.
*   **Symmetry Breaking:** In a simple 2-state/2-signal/2-act game, the dynamics invariably lead to one of the two efficient signaling systems.
    *   The "Principle of Sufficient Reason" is defeated by symmetry breaking.
    *   The population moves to a signaling system "as if guided by an unseen hand."

### 5. LEARNING (REINFORCEMENT)
*   **Roth-Erev / Herrnstein Learning:** Agents act based on accumulated rewards (weights).
*   **Result:** Reinforcement learning in simple signaling games converges to a signaling system with probability 1. Agents don't need to "know" they are playing a game.

### 6. COMPLEXITY & NETWORKS
*   **Mismatch (States vs. Signals):**
    *   *Too many signals:* Synonyms evolve.
    *   *Too few signals:* Information bottlenecks. Efficient partitions evolve.
*   **Networks:**
    *   *Multiple Senders:* Logic problems for the receiver (e.g., "A AND B" vs "A OR B").
    *   *Chains:* Information passed along (A -> B -> C).
    *   *Logic:* Even unsophisticated learning can solve these complex topological problems.

### 7. CONCLUSION
*   Meaningless signals can spontaneously acquire meaning through adaptive dynamics (evolution or learning).
*   This validates the Democratean view of convention arising from chance and habit.
