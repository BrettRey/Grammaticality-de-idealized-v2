### Structured review

**1. One-sentence summary.** The paper argues that grammaticality is not a single competence fact but a conditioned stability state, G̃_t = map · K · C_t, whose categorical appearance emerges from Bayesian licensing dynamics under preemption, and that this state is a real linguistic kind because it supports predictions about repair, satiation, transmission, and change.

**2. Strengths (projectibility dimension)**

- §projectible-G (ll. 886–909) makes exactly the move most HPC-adjacent papers omit: it claims the macro-state supports generalizations its component factors don't, closes the micro–macro loop (production and repair at t+1 depend on G̃_t, feeding the evidence streams s, e, p), and names the projectible generalizations (satiation profiles, transmission fidelity, repair rates, change trajectories). The kind claim at l. 1239 states the three-clause slogan almost verbatim.
- The Predictions subsection (ll. 1320–1367) derives three *discriminating* predictions (satiation-framing asymmetry, artificial-language opportunity-set manipulation with positive evidence held at zero, L2 rejection tracking L1 preemption mass rather than L2 frequency) that follow specifically from separating positive evidence, opportunity structure, and counterfactual choice, i.e., predictions rival frameworks don't make. The CxG and UBA comparisons are correctly framed as predictive advantage, not finer decomposition.
- Falsification conditions are wired in (§identification-c, ll. 983–986): three named dissociations that would show the state assignment wrong. This is empirical vulnerability, not just mechanism description.

**3. Weaknesses (specific, actionable)**

- **The projection claim is inconsistent with the model's own formalism.** §projectible-G says satiation profiles and change trajectories project "over G̃_t as such," "regardless of whether the low score comes from mapping failure, operator-value incoherence, or non-licensing" (ll. 889–894, 907–909). But the flagship prediction (ll. 1332–1342) turns on *evidence structure*: low-evidence forms satiate, high-preemption forms don't. G̃_t is a product of posterior *means*; Beta(1,3) and Beta(100,300) licensing posteriors have identical means and identical G̃, yet the model's own update rule (eq. at ll. 1159–1161) makes them behave differently under new exposure. The worked examples tacitly concede this ("C_t → 0 *with high certainty*," l. 1050). Either the projectible macro-state is the posterior (mean plus concentration), or the "as such" claim overreaches. As written, a careful reviewer can construct a counterexample to the kind claim from the paper's own equations. Fix: state which projections run over G̃_t alone (repair, per the equation at l. 901) and which need the finer state (satiation, actuation); that two-level story would *strengthen* the kind claim.
- **The front and back matter don't carry the claim.** The abstract has zero prediction language and frames the contribution as deriving categoricality from dynamics; the conclusion opens and closes on "understanding" with the predictive cash-out confined to one paragraph (l. 1405); "projectible" first appears past the halfway point with no gloss and no citation, and the "real linguistic kind" claim (l. 1239) engages no kinds literature at all (no Boyd, no Goodman; for a Glossa readership "projectible" needs the standard gloss and anchor; flag: I'm citing Boyd/Goodman as the conventional anchors from the framework given to me, not from a fresh read of either source). Fix: one sentence in the abstract stating a distinctive prediction, and promote the l. 1239 kind claim to the conclusion's frame.
- **Structural burial plus an amputated operational section.** Predictions is the *sixth* subsection of Theoretical implications, after five "Relationship to X" subsections; the reader receives the taxonomy (§2), the formalism (§3), the dynamics (§4), and five comparative discussions before reaching what the framework predicts. Meanwhile the Methodological Considerations section that operationalized the projections is commented out (ll. 1381–1397) while l. 1407 still references it. That dangling reference is a build-level bug, and a symptom: the operational payoff was cut, the mechanism material stayed. Fix: restore or replace the methodology content, and move Predictions ahead of (or interleave it with) the relationship subsections.

**4. Key question for Q&A.** "Your satiation prediction distinguishes low-evidence forms from strongly preempted forms; your projection base G̃_t, a product of posterior means, cannot. Which is the projectible macro-state, G̃_t 'as such' (§3.3.2), or the licensing posterior with its concentration, and if the latter, what survives of the claim that the generalizations project regardless of the source of the low score?"

**5. Verdict: Revise & Resubmit.** The third clause of the slogan is present and partly integrated, which is rare, but the abstract/intro/conclusion still sell mechanisms, and the paper's central projectibility claim is, as formalized, contradicted by its own flagship prediction; both are fixable without new machinery.

Minor notes outside my lane, flagged for the parent: the paper says "MVMG" throughout while the project's CLAUDE.md says the model is now OVMG; the abstract uses "The present framework" (house-style violation); the GrodnerGibson2005 "59–73% of variance" figure (l. 430) and the verkerk2025 counts (l. 1255) are the kind of specific statistics that need source verification before submission.

## Reviewer 2: Generative syntactician (hostile)

Reviewer background: generative syntax; experimental syntax sympathetic to Sprouse and Snyder.

I have read the full manuscript (all sections, formal core, dynamics module, appendix). Per Rapoport's Rules, I begin with a restatement the author should accept, then agreements, then criticism.

**Restatement of the position.** The paper argues that grammaticality, properly de-idealized, is not membership in a set generated by an autonomous competence grammar but a conditioned stability property of form-value relations within communicative situations. It factorizes into three state variables: map(u,c), a categorical analyzability predicate; K(u,c), graded coherence of grammaticalized operator values (with a null-outcome softmax so that a transparent but operator-violating construal drives K toward 0); and C_t(u,c), the population licensing rate. The graded score G̃ = map·K·C_t, thresholded by a decision-theoretic τ(c), yields categorical membership; a separate feeling model F handles gradient judgments, satiation, and misattribution. Categorical-seeming judgments emerge when Beta-posterior licensing dynamics become bimodal: preemption mass p_t = N_t·ρ* (opportunity volume times counterfactual choice probability) drives C_t toward an absorbing state near 0 or 1. Left-branch extraction (*Which did you buy car?*, ex. 1g) is thus a "stable gap": high-opportunity, fully preempted, non-satiating, requiring no structural veto. Independent relative *whose* (ex. 1d) is instead low-evidence indeterminacy: tiny opportunity set, so the posterior stays diffuse. The paper offers three prospective tests: framing-sensitive satiation for low-evidence forms but not stable gaps, artificial-language learning separating zero-evidence-high-opportunity from zero-evidence-low-opportunity, and L2 transfer tracking L1 preemption mass.

**Points of agreement, and what I learned.** The F/G separation (§ "The subjective experience of grammaticality", § "feeling-new") is correct and well executed; it is, as the paper half-admits in its competence/performance discussion (§ Theoretical implications, fourth point), a re-derivation of the competence/performance distinction with a better measurement theory attached. Second, the licensing-versus-selection and frequency-per-opportunity distinctions (§ "usage", § "Relationship to usage-based approaches") are a genuine advance over raw-frequency usage-based accounts; the demonstration that independent relative *whose* is "dramatically rarer than one would expect given the frequency of its component parts" is the right way to argue against naive entrenchment stories, and I will use it. Third, the identification section (§ "Identifying the conditioning state before prediction") shows more falsificationist discipline than is usual in this literature, and the treatment of *colorless green ideas* as K-preserving (the clash "doesn't mis-set an operator value", § value-compatibility) is an elegant way to keep Chomsky's 1957 point inside a usage architecture.

**1. One-sentence summary**

The paper proposes that grammaticality is a thresholded product of structural analyzability, operator-value coherence, and population-level licensing, with apparently categorical constraints such as the English left-branch extraction ban reanalyzed as "stable gaps" in which Bayesian licensing posteriors are driven to zero by preemption in high-opportunity niches rather than by structural prohibition.

**2. Strengths**

- The objective-status/subjective-feeling separation, with distinct evidence streams for each (C_t from corpus-rate-per-opportunity, production, and repair; F from ratings and online measures), is a real methodological contribution and blocks the crudest circularity in acceptability-based theorizing.
- The rare-versus-preempted contrast (Figure 1's two trajectories; the *whose* vs. LBE comparison in § "Situational entrenchment and acceptance") sharpens usage-based theory considerably: absence is evidential only in proportion to counterfactual choice probability. The critique of simple UBA frequency accounts in § "Relationship to usage-based approaches" is the best passage in the paper.
- The prediction menu (satiation framing, artificial-language learning with opportunity-set manipulation, L2 preemption transfer) is concrete by the standards of emergentist work, and the artificial-language design is actually runnable.

**3. Weaknesses**

**(a) The preemption mechanism fails precisely at the flagship case, and the paper's own partial-pooling machinery makes it worse.** Preemption in Goldberg's sense requires a near-synonymous competitor consistently chosen in contexts where the target would be expected; it works for *sheeps*/*sheep* and arguably for *I have 25 years* / *I am 25 years old*. But for LBE the niche-identity assumption is doing unexamined work. If "Which car did you buy?" and "Which did you buy car?" occupy the same niche, then note what follows cross-linguistically: in languages where LBE is grammatical, it stably coexists with the pied-piped variant, typically with an information-structural division of labour [I have not verified the Slavic facts against sources here; the Serbo-Croatian coexistence claim and Bošković's generalization linking LBE availability to article-lessness need verification, but if they hold they are devastating]. Coexistence of same-niche synonyms is exactly what preemption dynamics forbid: the entrenched competitor should have driven ρ*-weighted mass against LBE there too. To save the Slavic facts, the author must say the two variants occupy different niches (focus on the modifier vs. neutral); but then in English, "Which car did you buy?" is not the same-niche competitor either, N_t·ρ* for the LBE niche collapses, and the "stable gap" reverts to the low-evidence indeterminacy profile of *whose*, wrongly predicting satiable uncertainty rather than hard rejection. The dilemma is sharpened by the paper's own token-licensing equation, C_t(u,c) = Σ_g λ_g(u,c)·C_t(g,c) (§ Objects), introduced precisely so that novel sentences inherit licensing from activated nodes; § "Niches" then says the LBE candidate is generated "by combining wh-fronting, modifier--noun packaging, and question formation," all of which are licensed high-C_t nodes. By the model's own pooling rule, LBE should inherit substantial C_t unless a dedicated LBE-configuration node with dominant λ is placed by hand, and nothing in the paper constrains node granularity or λ_g. Finally, the model happily assimilates *I have 25 years* and *Which did you buy car?* to the same C_t→0 mechanism (compare Worked Example 4 with § "Relationship to Construction Grammar"), yet these differ empirically: extraction constraints cluster typologically, correlate with independent grammatical properties, and show super-additive factorial signatures [Sprouse-style factorial island results; flag: not verified against source here], while age-predication frames vary idiosyncratically language by language exactly as conventions should. That nobody has ever needed a "Left Age Condition" while the Left Branch Condition recurs across unrelated article languages is the datum the preemption story leaves as a coincidence.

**(b) The acquisition dynamics predict a developmental stage that does not exist, and the learning-from-absence problem is relocated, not solved.** Figure 1 starts both trajectories at prior mean 0.5000: an evidence-less learner is at chance on LBE. The model therefore predicts an early period in which children treat LBE as a live option at non-trivial rates, declining gradually as preemption mass accumulates. To my knowledge children produce essentially no LBE-type errors and reject such extractions early [flag: needs verification against the acquisition literature; the paper itself cites only Snyder 2022: 22 for the adult pattern]; Snyder's satiation results (no satiation for LBE, unlike whether-islands) are consistent with the paper's stable-gap taxonomy but not with its trajectory [flag: Snyder 2000 details need verification]. The paper owes us numbers: what N_t and ρ* must a three-year-old accumulate, over which niche individuation, to cross below τ(c) by age 4? None are given; Figure 1's x-axis is unlabeled "experience (steps)." Worse, the candidate set 𝒱* is "analogically generated" by the hierarchy ℋ with utilities U = θᵀf(v;n,c); every restriction on which candidates ℋ generates, and which features enter f, is unargued-for innate structure. The poverty-of-stimulus question has been moved into ℋ and θ, not answered.

**(c) Under-identification: every quantity that separates the diagnostic categories is latent, and the paper's falsification clause insulates the framework.** The classifications in the decision tree (§ "Diagnosing (un)grammaticality at a glance") and Worked Examples turn on ρ*, N_t, niche individuation, λ_g, node granularity, the open-ended constraint inventory 𝒥(c), τ(c), and the construal posterior q_h(c|e), none of which is measured or fixed in advance for any case in the paper. § "Identification" states that when predictions fail, "the state assignment is wrong", which is precisely the escape hatch: anomalies falsify auxiliary assignments, never the model. The K/C_t boundary illustrates the problem. The paper assigns *I've finished it yesterday* to K (Worked Example 2, K≈0, C_t≈1) and *I have 25 years* to C_t (§ value-compatibility: "not a K failure in the narrow sense"), but it also concedes that "a value can enter E only after the community has conventionalized it as a grammaticalized constraint" (§ Objects, energy model). Then φ_temp is itself a licensing fact; varieties in which perfect-plus-past-adverbial is acceptable [flag: Australian/Irish English claims need verification] confirm it is community-relative, and K collapses into feature-level C_t, leaving the two-way case assignment as bookkeeping by intuition. Note also the internal grain inconsistency: the Turkish appendix sets C_t = 1 for ungrammatical *kitap-ler* ("every speaker knows how plural and past are expressed"), reading C_t at the node level, while Worked Example 4 reads C_t at the token level for LBE. The same freedom that lets *kitap-ler* keep C_t = 1 would let LBE keep it too. And the stable-gap diagnostic "no intermediate ratings" (§ Apparent categorical gaps, criterion 2) is contradicted both by gradient island-violation ratings in the experimental literature [flag: verify] and by the paper's own noise term ε_i, which guarantees rating variance. Actionable remedy: pre-register, for a fixed list of 15-20 constructions, the estimation procedure for N_t and ρ* from corpus and elicitation data alone, and show the model recovers the stable-gap/rare/K-failure taxonomy without consulting the judgments it is meant to explain. Until then, § "Introduction"'s complaint that the competence/performance distinction "leads to circularity" (citing Schütze) applies with equal force here, merely with more parameters. The three-layer answer to the circularity objection the paper raises against the pure community-verdict view (§ f-m-pair-in-community) helps only if map and Λ are independently characterizable; but map presupposes a grammar of categories and composition (whose? that grammar is the generativist's explanandum, smuggled in as a primitive), and Λ_i, "licenses u as a community resource", is operationalized partly by "recognition as a legitimate resource" (§ Measurement model), which is a judgment. The author's own marginal note querying "objective grammaticality" given τ(c) suggests he already sees the soft spot.

**4. Key question for Q&A**

For a language in which left-branch extraction and pied-piping stably coexist, state the independently measurable property of the niche structure or of ρ* that prevents the frequent pied-piped variant from preempting LBE there, while guaranteeing that "Which car did you buy?" accumulates preemption mass against LBE in English, without consulting the acceptability judgments the model is supposed to predict. And as a corollary: what does the model predict a three-year-old English learner's C_t for LBE to be, and where in CHILDES is the corresponding tolerance stage?

**5. Verdict: Revise & Resubmit (major revisions).**

The F/G separation and the per-opportunity evidence framework deserve publication, but the flagship stable-gap analysis is under-identified, contradicted by its own pooling equation, and weakest exactly at the case (LBE) chosen to carry the argument against structural constraints; the paper should either supply pre-committed measurement procedures for ρ* and N_t or retreat to the cases (blocking, phraseological frames) where preemption is independently plausible.

*Source-grounding note: claims above marked [flag] (Slavic LBE coexistence, Bošković's article generalization, Snyder 2000 satiation specifics, children's non-production of LBE, factorial island gradience, perfect-plus-adverbial varieties) are from reviewer memory and must be verified against the primary literature before being relied on in a revision letter; the paper's own content is cited from the manuscript directly.*

## Reviewer 3: Usage-based / Construction Grammar linguist

**Reviewer stance:** usage-based / Construction Grammar, sympathetic to statistical preemption.

**Preamble (understanding check).** The paper's position, as I understand it: grammaticality is not string-set membership but conditioned stability in form-value relations, factored as G̃ₜ = map · K · Cₜ (structural analyzability, coherence of operator values under a null-outcome softmax, and population licensing rate). Licensing is learned by a Beta-posterior update in which non-occurrence counts as evidence only in proportion to "effective preemption mass" (opportunity count Nₜ times counterfactual choice probability ρ*). Categoricality is emergent bimodality of the licensing posterior, and it should cluster on closed-paradigm, high-opportunity, update-critical "operator" contrasts. Judgments (F) are a separate, noisier metacognitive signal. This is a framework I largely share premises with: no autonomous syntax module, conventions as community facts, preemption as the engine of apparent bans. I also learned something from it: the whose-vs-LBE contrast (tiny opportunity set yielding uncertainty vs. large opportunity set yielding confident rejection) is a cleaner way to distinguish "rare" from "absent" than anything I had articulated, and the decision-theoretic threshold τ(c) is a neat account of situation-dependent judgment strictness.

### 1. One-sentence summary

The paper proposes that grammaticality is conditioned stability in form-value relations (map · K · Cₜ), with categorical judgments emerging when Beta-posterior licensing dynamics, driven by effective preemption mass (opportunities × counterfactual choice probability), push constructions toward bimodal licensed/unlicensed attractors, predicting that categorical policing concentrates on closed-paradigm, high-opportunity operator contrasts.

### 2. Strengths

- **The effective preemption mass formalization (§7.11–7.12) is a real contribution to preemption theory.** pₜ = Σ ρ*(u | n, c) makes mathematically explicit what Goldberg (2011) argued informally, that absence is evidence only where the form would otherwise be expected, and it derives, rather than stipulates, the difference between low-evidence uncertainty (independent relative *whose*) and high-certainty rejection (left-branch extraction). Figure 1 and the four worked examples (§7.10) show the machinery earning its keep. [Goldberg 2011 read only as cited in the paper; verify against source.]
- **The identification and falsification discipline (§7.7, §7.9) is better than most usage-based statements of community-relative grammaticality.** Cₜ is identified by corpus-rate-per-opportunity, elicited production, and repair; F by ratings and online measures; and the paper names the dissociations that would falsify the state assignment. This genuinely blocks the "sympathetic norm-centre chosen after the fact" escape that plagues situation-relative accounts.
- **The predictions section is concrete and discriminating.** Satiation-by-framing (dialect-resource framing should help *whose* but not LBE), the artificial-language design holding positive evidence at zero while manipulating opportunity-set size, and L2 rejection tracking L1 preemption mass are all falsifiable and would distinguish this model from a flat frequency account. These are experiments I would actually want to run.

### 3. Weaknesses

- **(W1) The claimed value-added over usage-based work is overstated, and the engagement with the relevant literature is thin at exactly the points where priority is claimed.** §8.4 says MVMG "adds two distinctions that usage-based work often leaves implicit: licensing versus selection, and raw frequency versus frequency per opportunity." Both have substantial precedent. Opportunity-relative frequency is built into statistical preemption from the start (the paper's own §2.3 gloss of Goldberg 2011 says "in contexts where the ungrammatical form would otherwise be expected"); Stefanowitsch's negative entrenchment computes observed-against-expected frequency, which just is frequency per opportunity [not cited, not in refs.bib; verify]; Ambridge's entrenchment/preemption/FIT debates bear directly on whether pₜ and eₜ are separable evidence streams [ambridge2020, AmbridgeGoldberg2008 sit unused in refs.bib; verify]; and variationist sociolinguistics has normalized by opportunity since Labov's principle of accountability (Labov 1972 is cited, but only for community norms). The individual/population split Λᵢ vs. Cₜ is Schmid's entrenchment-vs-conventionalization distinction [schmid2020 in refs.bib, uncited; verify], and the population licensing dynamics descend from Croft's utterance-selection theory (Baxter/Blythe/Croft models are cited; Croft 2000 itself is not). The λ_g "coverage weights" reproduce Goldberg's (2019) coverage without attribution at the point of use. **Action:** rewrite §8.3–8.4 to credit these lineages and restate the delta precisely. The honest claim is strong enough: MVMG's novelty is the *joint formalization* (licensing/selection factorization of usage rate, preemption mass as N × ρ*, Beta dynamics yielding bimodality) plus the clustering prediction, not the distinctions themselves.
- **(W2) "Operator" does no independent predictive work in this paper, and the paper is not self-contained on it.** The prediction is that categorical policing targets "closed-paradigm, high-opportunity, update-critical" contrasts. But closure and opportunity are the very parameters that drive bimodality in §7.12, so the derivation goes through without "update-critical" ever being consulted. The one place the functional criterion is stress-tested, gender concord (§8.7), is conceded via "paradigm inertia," an escape hatch. And the paper's flagship categorical example, *I have 25 years*, is analyzed as non-licensing of an age-predication *frame*, a lexically specific construction that is not obviously a closed-paradigm operator contrast, so its categorical rejection is predicted by opportunity-plus-preemption alone, with operatorhood idle. Everything that would let a reviewer evaluate operatorhood (the K inventory in §7.2 repeatedly defers to "grammaticalized features live in c" and "closed accountable repertoires") is outsourced to reynolds2026operators. As it stands, "operator" reads as a relabeling of "grammaticalized/closed-class" with a functional gloss attached. **Action:** either (a) give within-paper criteria for operatorhood that are independent of Nₜ and ρ*, plus at least one contrast case (closed-paradigm, high-opportunity, *not* update-critical) where MVMG predicts weaker policing than a closed-class account would; or (b) demote the operator claim to the companion paper and let this paper predict what its mathematics actually delivers: categoricality tracks effective preemption mass.
- **(W3) The situation-relativity evidence conflates licensing with accommodation, and the showcase empirical claim is under-reported.** (i) *Ava cookie* (§4.1.1): caregivers tolerating and interpreting the child's utterance is acceptance-as-accommodation, not licensing-as-resource. On the paper's own measurement model (§7.9), caregiver recasts (repair behaviour) would identify Cₜ ≈ 0 in the home situation too, and §7.7 explicitly forbids rescuing a form by post-hoc choice of a sympathetic norm-centre, which is what this example does. The code-mixing example is far better (Toribio's judgment data show systematic constraints, so bilingual norms are doing real licensing work), but the aside about intransitive gym *lifting* slides from operator licensing to lexical/register convention, precisely the grammar/style line the model is supposed to draw. Cut or rework Ava; keep and sharpen the bilingual case. (ii) The *whose* corpus claim: "A search of the 1-billion-word [COCA] returned no instances" reports no query strings, no operationalization of independent relative genitive *whose* against interrogative and dependent *whose* (a nontrivial search problem), and no hand-checking protocol. Most tellingly for a model whose central quantities are Nₜ and ρ*, "the opportunity set is also tiny" is asserted, never estimated. One rough worked estimate of the niche frequency would convert the model's showcase contrast from schematic to empirical. Also fix "(Reynolds under review)" appearing as bare text, and reconcile the two characterizations of hankamer1973whose (§4.1.6 "deem it non-existent" vs. §8.2 "violates no syntactic principles") against the source [verify].

**Minor points.** The abstract's claim that CxG hasn't "adequately explained why semantically coherent constructions may nonetheless be deemed ungrammatical" overclaims; preemption is exactly CxG's answer, and the paper itself adopts it. On "value": CxG's function side already includes information structure, discourse pragmatics, and social meaning, so much of *valeur* is redundant with constructional meaning; the genuinely additive part is paradigmatic contrast, which is what powers preemption, and the paper should say so explicitly or drop the Saussure framing. Relatedly, the model's own name ("Morphosyntactic-Value") contradicts §8.3's insistence that the operator boundary is not substance-based. Figure 1's update parameters are unstated (the curves imply per-step preemption masses of roughly 0.1 vs. 5; report them). The "59–73% of variance" locality figure attributed to Grodner & Gibson (2005) needs source verification. The satiation literature on islands (Snyder's work; Chaves and colleagues on improvement with exposure) should be engaged before claiming LBE "resists satiation" [verify]. Bib hygiene: Goldberg2011 and goldberg2011preemption look like duplicate entries. [Parent's note: checked — this is a biber `keyalias`, a single entry with an alias, not a duplicate.]

### 4. Key question (Q&A)

If closure and opportunity already suffice to derive bimodal licensing, what observation would distinguish "categorical policing targets operator contrasts" from "categorical policing targets whatever has high effective preemption mass"? Concretely: name a closed-paradigm, high-opportunity contrast that is *not* update-critical, and tell us whether your model predicts categorical policing for it, because that is the only case where the operator layer and the dynamics come apart.

### 5. Verdict

**Revise & Resubmit (major).** The preemption-mass formalization and the predictions are a genuine advance worth publishing in a general venue, but the novelty claims against existing usage-based work need recalibration (W1), the operator prediction needs independent traction or honest demotion (W2), and the flagship empirical claims need reportable methods (W3).

**Source-grounding note for the author/editor:** in this review I have read only main.tex and checked refs.bib; my characterizations of Goldberg (2011, 2019), Stefanowitsch's negative entrenchment, Ambridge's FIT/entrenchment work, Schmid (2020), Croft (2000), Robenalt & Goldberg, and the island-satiation literature are from a reviewer's working memory and should each be verified against the sources before any revision responds to them.

## Reviewer 4: Experimental-syntax methodologist

**Reviewer profile:** experimental-syntax methodologist / quantitative linguist

### 1. One-sentence summary

The paper proposes that objective grammaticality is conditioned stability in form–value relations, formalized as G̃_t(u,c) = map(u,c)·K(u,c)·C_t(u,c) with a separate subjective anomaly signal F_i,t, and derives apparent categorical gaps (e.g., left-branch extraction) not from hard representational bans but from Beta-posterior licensing dynamics in which "effective preemption mass" (opportunities × counterfactual choice probability) drives community licensing toward an absorbing zero.

Before criticizing, let me register what I take the paper to be doing right at the design level, because the architecture is better than most of what this literature offers. The state-theory/dynamics separation (§"A formal core" vs. §"Dynamics") is clean; the licensing-vs.-selection distinction (π_t ≈ C_t·ρ*_t, §"Usage as licensing × choice") is a genuine advance over raw-frequency usage-based accounts; and I learned something from the effective-preemption-mass construct: weighting absence by counterfactual choice probability is the crispest formalization of "structured non-occurrence" I have seen, and it makes the *whose*-vs.-LBE contrast (tiny opportunity set vs. high preemption mass) empirically meaningful rather than hand-waved.

### 2. Strengths

- **Opportunity-normalized evidence is the right move.** The insistence that raw frequency has to be normalized by opportunity set, and that absence is evidential only in proportion to ρ*_t (§"Dynamics: preemption as effective preemption mass"), correctly separates "rare because the niche is rare" (*a friend of whose*) from "absent despite abundant opportunity" (LBE). This dissolves a real confound in usage-based argumentation, and the paper is honest that this is where its distinctive content lives.
- **Measurement-theoretic ambition.** Treating C_t as latent, identified by converging non-rating indicators (§"Measurement model for C_t"), with F identified by a separate battery (§"Identifying the conditioning state before prediction"), is exactly the right aspiration, and the temperature/thermometer analogy in §"Distinguishing objective grammaticality from subjective ratings" frames it well. Few papers in this area even attempt an explicit measurement model.
- **A falsifying observation is named.** The claim that satiation changing *later corpus rates* for a purportedly stable gap would be disconfirming is a concrete, checkable commitment, and the three proposed studies (satiation framing, artificial-language opportunity manipulation, L2 preemption transfer) are real designs, not gestures.

### 3. Weaknesses

**(W1) The identifiability story leaks at every joint, and the falsifiability paragraph quietly undoes itself.**
The paper claims C_t is identified by "corpus-rate-per-opportunity, elicited production, and repair behaviour, not by acceptability ratings alone" (§"Identifying the conditioning state"). But by the paper's own equations, none of these identifies C_t:

- Corpus rate per opportunity is π_t ≈ C_t·ρ*_t. This identifies a *product*. ρ*_t is a softmax over utilities U_t = θᵀf(v;n,c) with free θ and unspecified features, and for unattested variants it is counterfactual by construction. "Unlicensed but choosable" (C≈0, ρ* high) and "licensed but disfavoured" (C high, ρ*≈0) both predict zero corpus rate. The theory's central quantity for stable gaps is thus exactly the one the corpus indicator cannot separate.
- Elicited production is Pr(produce) ∝ G̃_t·ρ*_t (§"Projectible use"), again a product, now also folding in K and map.
- Repair is Pr(repair) = r₀ + r₁(1−G̃_t), a function of G̃, not C_t, and behaviourally, repair is plausibly mediated by the same metacognitive anomaly signal F that drives ratings. No argument is given that repair bypasses the F channel. If it doesn't, the C-battery and the F-battery share a common cause and the claimed dissociation test loses its force.
- The fourth indicator, "recognition as a legitimate resource," is an acceptability judgment in all but name.

Worse, the falsifiability sentence reads: "The model is falsified where these dissociate in the wrong direction: if satiation changes later corpus rates for a purportedly stable gap ... **the state assignment is wrong**." That last clause converts falsification into reassignment: the observation impeaches the token's classification, not MVMG. Combined with the flexibility of c (𝒞 has no fixed granularity; c can be "learned, split, and renegotiated"; τ(c), κ_c, 𝒥(c), and the w_j all condition on c), the theory as stated can absorb any dissociation by re-partitioning. §"Identifying the conditioning state" makes the right gesture (fix c from non-judgment cues *before* prediction), but there is no procedure for fixing the *partition* of 𝒞, no stopping rule for splitting c, and no statement of what recurrent reassignment would cost the theory. **Actionable:** commit to (i) a pre-registered conditioning partition per study, (ii) an independent estimation protocol for ρ*_t (e.g., forced-choice norming over the competitor set, fit before the licensing data are touched), and (iii) an explicit rule for when repeated state-reassignment counts against MVMG itself.

**(W2) The formal layer has several outright glitches.**

- *F's range is inconsistent with its equation.* §"The feeling of (un)grammaticality" declares F_i,t ∈ [−1,0], but the right-hand side −α(1−G̃) − γᵀR_i − δD_i + ε_i is unbounded below (L(u) sums over arbitrarily many dependencies; the sublinear saturation caps growth *rate*, not the sum) and, with ε_i unrestricted, can exceed 0, contradicting the stated interpretation that F=0 is null affect and there is no positive feeling of grammaticality. A squashing link or truncation is needed, and "ratings mapped linearly to [−1,0]" then becomes a misspecified measurement rule. Also, the dependency index i in L(u) = Σᵢ ℓ(|dᵢ|) collides with the individual index i in F_i,t.
- *logit(C_t) blows up at the paper's own stated values.* The measurement model y = b + Λ·logit(C_t) + ε is undefined at C_t = 0 and C_t = 1, yet worked example 4 asserts "C_t → 0" for stable gaps, and the Turkish appendix asserts C_t(u,c) = 1 exactly (twice). The Beta dynamics rescue this internally (posterior means never reach the boundary in finite time), but then the prose must stop asserting exact boundary values, or the model needs a different link.
- *Node/token slippage in the appendix.* C_t is defined as Pr_i(Λ_i,t(u,c)=1), the rate at which individuals license *u*. The appendix assigns C_t = 1 to \*kitap-ler, a word no Turkish speaker licenses. What is meant is that the *plural node* is licensed and K ≈ 0 does the work; as written, it contradicts the definition. Worked example 2 (\**I've finished it yesterday*, "C_t ≈ 1, components entrenched") has the same slippage, defensible under partial pooling but not as notated.
- *The mean-field note doesn't match the plotted dynamics.* The boxed note claims the Beta update is approximated by the logistic ODE Ċ = rC(1−C). But the deterministic update actually plotted decays hyperbolically: I reverse-engineered the figure coordinates, and both curves are exactly C_t = 1/(2 + p·t), i.e., Beta(1,1) prior with constant per-step preemption mass p = 0.1 (blue) and p = 5 (red); every plotted value checks out (e.g., blue t=10: 1/3 = 0.3333; red t=1: 1/7 = 0.1429). So the figure is *internally consistent with the stated update*, to the paper's credit. But 1/(2+pt) is a decaying-gain stochastic-approximation trajectory, not a logistic; the logistic form requires constant gain, i.e., discounting/forgetting of old evidence, which is nowhere in the model. Since the bimodality argument in §"Emergent categoricality" leans on the logistic fixed-point structure, this is not cosmetic.
- *The figure undercuts the text's own contrast.* Both curves converge to zero; the "Rare (low opps)" curve is merely slower. After ~50 steps the blue trace sits at 0.145 with cumulative preemption mass ≈ 4.9, which is not the "little evidence either way ... high uncertainty rather than confident rejection" that §"Situational entrenchment" claims for *whose*. If the *whose* story is near-zero preemption mass, the blue curve should be approximately flat at the prior with wide posterior variance, and the figure should plot credible intervals, because on the paper's own account the rare/gap contrast is a *variance* contrast, not (only) a mean contrast. As drawn, the difference between "indeterminate" and "categorically rejected" is a parameter and a horizon, not a different attractor.
- *Source-grounding flag:* the claim that locality accounts for "59–73% of variance in reading times" (§"Dependency locality", citing Grodner & Gibson 2005) needs verification against the source; I could not check it (the paper is not in the accessible literature set), and a variance-explained figure of that size for a single predictor in SPR data would be unusual enough to double-check both the number and what it is variance *of* (condition means vs. trial-level RTs matters enormously here). [Parent's note: verified against the PDF in ~/Downloads — see synthesis.]

**(W3) Target heterogeneity: Pr_i(Λ_i,t = 1) is the wrong object for some of the phenomena the paper itself describes, and the state theory contradicts the dynamics module.**
Credit first: the framework disaggregates more heterogeneity sources than any rival I know: diachronic change gets t, context-sensitivity gets c, construal ambiguity gets K's null-outcome race, prescriptive overlay gets D_i, and noise gets ε. That is genuinely good. But the residue matters:

- Λ_i,t ∈ {0,1} forces every individual to be a licensor or a non-licensor, so C_t = 0.5 cannot distinguish a *stable mixture* (half the community licenses *might could*, half never will) from *shared individual uncertainty* (everyone on the fence about *whose*). These are different kinds, with different projectible profiles (the mixture predicts bimodal individual-level judgment distributions, high test–retest consistency within speakers, and sociolinguistic conditioning; shared uncertainty predicts unimodal mid-scale ratings and low within-speaker consistency). Individual-level repeated-measures designs distinguish them routinely; the model's central quantity erases the distinction.
- The paper's own prose knows this: §"Situational entrenchment" says of *whose* that "some speakers can make the analogical leap ... others remain unsure; still others ... can't construct a stable form--value relation at all." That is a three-component mixture with within-speaker gradience, which binary Λ cannot encode. Meanwhile, the dynamics module gives each learner a *graded* Beta posterior. So individuals are graded in §"Dynamics" and binary in §"Objects." Pick one: either Λ_i,t is a thresholded read-out of an individual-level graded state (then say so, and let C_t be a population functional of the individual posteriors, which also gives you the mixture/uncertainty distinction for free), or the state theory stays binary and loses the phenomena.
- The escape hatch of splitting c ("stable within-c disagreement means c was mis-drawn") reconnects to W1: without a stopping rule, every stable disagreement can be redescribed as two communities, and "objective grammaticality relative to c" trivializes. If the population rate is nonetheless the right object because grammaticality is community convention (a defensible position!), the paper should argue that explicitly and say what C_t = 0.6 *means*: the τ(c) thresholding device answers a decision question, not the constitution question, and the paper elsewhere endorses both-parties-right variation (§"Operator-valued form--value relations").
- Relatedly, for a Glossa-tier audience the paper needs contact with the quantitative acceptability-measurement literature it is implicitly rebuilding: test–retest reliability of judgments, individual-difference decompositions, and existing latent-variable/probabilistic models of gradient acceptability (the Sprouse–Schütze–Almeida line; Langsford et al.; Lau, Clark & Lappin's probabilistic grammaticality). I flag these from field knowledge, not from checked sources, but their absence from a paper proposing a *measurement model for grammaticality* will strike every methodologist reviewer.

**On the experiments** (folding into W1/W3 rather than a fourth bullet): the artificial-language design is the strongest and closest to genuinely diagnostic, since it holds positive evidence at zero while manipulating opportunity structure; but its predicted direction is shared by the whole rational-inference/indirect-negative-evidence family (suspicious-coincidence and entrenchment-style accounts; the Perfors–Tenenbaum line, claim needing verification against sources), so it discriminates MVMG from categorical-ban accounts, not from its Bayesian siblings, and the paper should say which named rival predicts the *opposite* cell. The satiation-framing prediction is confounded as designed: LBE starts near floor and *whose* mid-scale, so the predicted framing × evidence-type interaction is mimicked by scale compression, regression toward the mid-point, and generic leniency response bias under "this is a dialect" instructions (which may change what question participants answer rather than the feeling itself); the repair/production DVs help, but the design needs baseline matching or latent-scale (e.g., ordinal IRT) analysis to be probative. The L2 prediction ("track L1 preemption mass, not L2 frequency") is distinctive only if L1 preemption mass can be estimated independently of the judgments it predicts, which returns us to the ρ* identification problem; otherwise full-transfer accounts predict the same sign.

### 4. Key question for Q&A

"Corpus rate per opportunity identifies the product C_t·ρ*_t, and for an unattested form ρ*_t is counterfactual by construction. Walk me through how you estimate ρ*_t for left-branch extraction without building in the conclusion; and since your falsifiability clause says a wrong-direction dissociation shows 'the state assignment is wrong,' tell me what pattern of data would count against MVMG itself rather than against a state assignment or a choice of c."

### 5. Verdict

**Revise & Resubmit (major).** The conceptual architecture (licensing vs. selection, opportunity-normalized preemption, state/dynamics separation) is a real contribution worth publishing, but the identification claims don't survive the paper's own equations, the formal layer has boundary-value and range inconsistencies that a copy-editor won't catch but a modeler will, and the population-rate construct erases exactly the mixture-vs-uncertainty distinction the paper's flagship examples require.

**Note on sources:** I verified the figure numerics against the stated model myself (they check out) and confirmed the GrodnerGibson2005 and gibson2026 entries exist in `refs.bib`; the 59–73% variance figure, the Perfors/Tenenbaum attribution, and the acceptability-measurement citations I suggest are flagged as requiring verification against the sources, which I did not read.

## Reviewer 5: Philosopher of science

*Reviewer: philosopher of science and language, for a Glossa-tier general linguistics journal*

### 1. One-sentence summary

The paper proposes that grammaticality is a conditioned, population-level state property, a graded stability score G̃_t(u,c) = map · K · C_t with categorical status derived by a situation-relative decision threshold τ(c), sharply distinguished from the individual's feeling of anomaly F, with an etiological dynamics module in which Beta-posterior licensing under opportunity-weighted preemption drives most construction nodes to bimodal attractors, so that apparent categorical gaps (left-branch extraction) are stable near-zero licensing states rather than representational bans, and grammaticality itself is "a real linguistic kind: a projectible profile of conditioned form--value stability."

Before criticizing (Rapoport): I take the paper's central insight to be that absence of positive evidence is only evidential in proportion to counterfactual choice probability times opportunity-set size, and that this single quantity (effective preemption mass, §5.3/eq. for p_t) derives the difference between *friend of whose* (rare, uncertain, satiable) and LBE (common niche, systematically untaken, categorical) without stipulating a grammar-internal ban. I agree with the two-layer G/F architecture, with the identification discipline in §4.7 (C_t from corpus-rate-per-opportunity, production, and repair; F from ratings and online measures; c fixed by non-judgment cues before prediction), and with the deflationary reading of emergence. The licensing-versus-selection distinction (§5.2) is something I learned from the paper and will use.

### 2. Strengths

- **The preemption formalization is a genuine advance over the informal usage-based literature.** p_t(u,c) = Σ ρ*_t(u | n,c) over N_t opportunities makes "absence as evidence" quantitative and opportunity-normalized, and it yields three distinctive, falsifiable predictions (§Predictions: satiation-framing asymmetry, artificial-language zero-evidence manipulation, L2 preemption transfer) that competing accounts do not make. Figure 1's rare-vs-stable-gap contrast follows from the model rather than being read into it.
- **The state/dynamics split is explicitly drawn and mostly policed.** §4 announces "the state theory is constitutive; the dynamics module is etiological," and §4.7 states falsification conditions (if satiation changes later corpus rates for a purported stable gap, the state assignment is wrong). The processing-to-diachrony bridge in §2.x is exemplary level discipline: the Fedorenko and locality evidence is downgraded, in the paper's own words, to "a plausibility claim about one pathway into conventional stability, not a direct neural explanation of grammatical change."
- **The emergence claim is as weak as advertised.** §Emergent categoricality derives population bimodality from the mean-field logistic dynamics (fixed points at 0 and 1, sign of the net evidence rate r) and explicitly distances itself from Hopper. Nothing spooky is smuggled in; this is aggregation, honestly labelled.

### 3. Weaknesses

**(i) "Objective grammaticality" overclaims, and the paper's own margin note knows it.** Two senses of "objective" are conflated. G̃_t is objective in the innocuous sense that it transcends any individual's response, the way facts about word meaning or money do (social kinds can be perfectly real; Khalidi-style arguments would help here, [verify specific source]). But categorical G_t = 𝕀[G̃_t ≥ τ(c)] inherits τ(c) = L_FA/(L_FA + L_FR), and the paper never says whose losses these are. If judge-relative, there is no fact of the matter about G_t within a fixed c; if a conventional feature of c, then categorical grammaticality is doubly conventional (conventional licensing rates cut by a conventional decision criterion), and the BRETT-VERIFY suggestion at line 562 ("conventional status") is the right move. Worse, the decision-theoretic motivation is quietly circular: losses for misclassifying an item as IN-REPERTOIRE presuppose an independent fact about repertoire membership, which the threshold was supposed to define. The fix is available inside the paper and goes unused: connect τ(c) to §Emergent categoricality. Where licensing posteriors are bimodal, G_t is threshold-insensitive and τ's arbitrariness is confined to the contested middle, where judgments are in fact unstable and stakes-sensitive; that is a prediction, not an embarrassment. Note also that §"Distinguishing objective grammaticality from subjective ratings" asserts that "the underlying grammatical system makes categorical distinctions," which contradicts the formal account, where categoricality is either thresholded or emergent.

**(ii) The three-factor decomposition is not as independent as "map · K · C_t" advertises, and the micro-macro bridge has an aggregation gap.** The paper concedes, twice, that K's constraint inventory 𝒥(c) is "partly derivative of licensing facts" and calls this a virtue. But map is also defined relative to a category system that is itself conventional (*Can the have running* fails to parse only given English's conventional categorizations of *can*, *the*, *have*). So all three variables are conventionality at different grains, and the multiplicative form is bookkeeping, not ontology. Concretely: what principled criterion books *I've finished it yesterday* as K ≈ 0, C_t ≈ 1 rather than as low pooled C_t for the composite type? C_t gets a measurement model (§4.9); K gets none, so the factorization is not identifiable from the paper's own evidence streams. Separately, the state theory defines C_t as a population rate of binary attitudes Λ_i, while the dynamics compute a single learner's Beta-posterior mean, and eq. (1) silently identifies the two. Disagreement (half the community confidently licenses) and shared uncertainty (everyone at credence 0.5) both come out C_t = 0.5, yet the *whose* analysis needs exactly that difference ("high uncertainty rather than confident rejection"). Finally, partial pooling creates a credit-assignment problem the paper doesn't address: an LBE candidate activates licensed wh-fronting and question-formation nodes, so token-level C_t = Σ λ_g C_t(g,c) should be substantially positive unless preemption mass concentrates on a gap-specific node; nothing says how λ_g achieves that (the "where does negative evidence attach" problem; cf. Bayesian preemption work such as Perfors, Tenenbaum & Wonnacott, [verify], uncited).

**(iii) The title-level promises, de-idealization and kindhood, are not cashed.** The idealization being removed is never named, quoted, or analyzed; the obvious target (the ideal speaker-listener in a completely homogeneous speech community, Chomsky 1965, [verify page 3]) appears only implicitly via a competence-performance citation. There is no engagement with the idealization literature (which idealizations were Galilean and harmless, which distorting; what tractability the de-idealized model gives up), nor with philosophy-of-linguistics work on the competence idealization (Nefdt, Santana, Devitt, [verify specific works]). The word "de-idealized" occurs three times, all in framing positions. Likewise, the natural-kind claim in §Theoretical implications arrives in one paragraph with no Boyd, no Khalidi, no Goodman gloss on projectibility, and a property/kind slippage (is *grammaticality* the kind, or are licensed form-value relations its members?). The materials for a real HPC argument are already in §Projectible use of the graded score (the production/repair feedback loop is the homeostatic mechanism; the repair/transmission/satiation/change profile is the cluster), but assembling them would force a concession the paper resists: if the kind's projectibility depends on the maintaining mechanism, the constitutive story is not cleanly separable from the etiological one after all. On circularity, the §"whatever the community accepts" reply defeats the *behaviourist* version of the objection (layering plus counterfactual opportunity structure adds empirical content) but not the *constitutive* one: Λ_i is defined as treating u "as a legitimate option," so grammaticality is defined via attitudes whose content is legitimacy. The available repair is Lewisian, conventions as self-referential equilibria, with the t-indexing turning the circle into a diachronic feedback loop; the paper gestures at this ("closes the micro--macro loop") but should make the move explicitly (Lewis 1969, [verify]).

### 4. Key question for Q&A

Take a form sitting at G̃_t = 0.4 in a fixed conditioning state c. Is there a fact of the matter about whether it is grammatical in c? If yes, what fixes τ(c) other than the classification behaviour you are trying to predict, and how would we measure τ(c) independently? If no, why keep "objective grammaticality" for G_t rather than reserving objectivity for the gradient and conceding that the categorical cut is conventional?

### 5. Verdict

**Revise & Resubmit.** The formal core, the preemption-mass machinery, and the predictions are publishable and better than anything comparable in the acceptability literature; but the philosophical vocabulary ("objective," "de-idealized," "real kind") currently outruns what the formalism licenses, the three-factor independence and the individual/population identification need repair, and none of these fixes threatens the architecture, which is exactly what an R&R is for.

**Minor notes for the author:** the author's own BRETT-VERIFY comments (line 562 on "conventional status," line 1196 on Keller's invisible hand, which is indeed the right citation for §Emergent categoricality) point in the directions this review presses; the abstract's claim that previous approaches "haven't adequately explained" preemption effects overstates novelty relative to Goldberg 2011/2019, which the model extends rather than supersedes; the probabilistic-grammaticality literature (Lau, Clark & Lappin, [verify]) is conspicuously absent; the central restriction on *which* contrasts attract categorical policing is offloaded to a companion preprint (reynolds2026operators), which reviewers at a general journal will want summarized in-text; and F ∈ [−1, 0] plus additive noise ε_i can exit the interval (cosmetic).
