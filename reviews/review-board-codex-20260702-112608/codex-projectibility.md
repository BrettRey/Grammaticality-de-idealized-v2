**1. Main Claim**
The paper argues that grammaticality is a projectible, situation-relative form-value profile: `map * K * C_t`, read through a separate feeling model, whose value lies in predicting repair, satiation, transmission, L2 transfer, and change.

**2. Strengths**
- The paper now has a genuinely projectibility-first spine. The abstract, intro, Figure 1, formal core, implications opener, predictions, and conclusion all make the same claim: grammaticality matters because it supports inferences, not because it names mechanisms.
- The best technical move is the distinction between low mean licensing and posterior concentration in “Projectible use of the graded score.” The `whose` vs. LBE contrast is not just descriptive; it predicts different satiation/framing behaviour.
- The support claims are graded carefully. Lines 1451 and 1662 avoid the common HPC failure of treating “homeostatic” as magic: stabilization, maintenance, and corrective control are separated, with control left open.

**3. Weaknesses**
- The production/repair equations in “Projectible use of the graded score” risk making `G-tilde` too projectible. Lines 925-932 say low `G-tilde` licenses the same expectations whether caused by mapping failure, incoherence, or non-licensing. Unstated premise: all low-score sources project similarly for production/repair rate. That likely needs narrowing to coarse repair probability, with failure-source-specific predictions kept separate.
- “Mechanisms of grammatical change” at lines 675-730 still reads partly like a mechanism catalogue: semantic, social, structural, iconic motivations. It should more consistently say what each mechanism predicts about repair, transmission, actuation, or stability, otherwise this local section briefly reverts to “here are mechanisms.”
- The threshold-reality test is strong but underwritten mainly as a research program. Lines 993-1010 and 1610-1626 make the categorical predicate depend on discontinuity at `tau(c)`. Unstated premise: `tau(c)` can be estimated independently enough that a discontinuity test is not circular. The paper acknowledges this, but the operational recipe needs to be sharper.

**4. Key Q&A Question**
What independent evidence would let us estimate `rho_t^*` and `tau(c)` before observing the relevant judgments, so that the model’s projective successes are not achieved by retrospectively fitting opportunity structure and threshold location?

**5. Verdict**
Accept, on projectibility grounds: projectibility is load-bearing across the paper, and mechanisms are mostly presented as explaining why the projections hold rather than as the contribution itself.
