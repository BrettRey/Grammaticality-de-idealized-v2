# Source hook: Ross (2026), research programmes as the unit of progress

**Source.** Lewis Ross, "Are Philosophers Absurd? Progress, Testimony and
Division of Labour," *Proc. Aristotelian Society* (advance article), DOI
10.1093/arisoc/aoag025, OA CC BY 4.0. Central note:
`../../../../literature/ross_2026_are_philosophers_absurd.notes.md`.

**Why this project cares.** Ross relocates the subject of progress from the
discipline to *research communities prosecuting a programme*, and confronts the
"climbing the mountain from different sides" convergence worry directly (he
calls the assumption that incompatible programmes all converge "heroic"). Both
touch open threads here: the un-integrated Powell contingency/convergence
connection, and the rival-conceptual-DAG workbench, which is precisely a set of
competing research communities modelling grammaticality, some of which are wrong
but which may be collectively efficient.

**Claim it may support.** The Zollman-style point Ross leans on (Mayo-Wilson,
Zollman & Danks 2011; Smart 2018) is a ready-made rationale for keeping rival
grammaticality DAGs alive rather than prematurely unifying: dispersing effort
across programmes, including likely-wrong ones, is structurally efficient.

**Claim it may challenge.** Ross's residual pessimism (most programmes are
probably wrong) is a caution against reading convergence between OVMG and rivals
as evidence of correctness; it may just be shared assumptions inside one
programme. Pairs with Powell: distinguish genuine convergence-under-constraint
from within-programme agreement.
